Ingrown Toenail | Nail Pain and Infection

An ingrown toenail (onychocryptosis) occurs when the corner or side of a toenail grows into the surrounding skin. This condition often affects the big toe and may lead to pain, swelling, redness, and infection if untreated. Proper foot hygiene, nail cutting techniques, and timely treatment prevent complications.

Symptoms

The common signs of an ingrown toenail include:

  • Pain and tenderness along the nail edges
  • Redness and swelling around the toe
  • Warmth in the affected area
  • Hardening of the skin near the nail
  • Drainage of pus or fluid if infection develops
  • Overgrowth of skin around the toenail (granulation tissue)
  • Difficulty walking or wearing shoes comfortably

Causes

Ingrown toenails usually occur due to improper nail cutting or repetitive pressure on the toe. Common causes include:

  • Cutting toenails too short or rounded
  • Wearing tight or narrow shoes
  • Toe injury or trauma
  • Genetic tendency (curved nails)
  • Poor foot hygiene
  • Repetitive stress (sports, running, long walking)
  • Nail infections or fungal infection weakening the nail

Precautions | Preventions

  • Cut nails straight across, not curved.
  • Avoid trimming nails too short.
  • Wear comfortable, breathable shoes with enough toe room.
  • Practice daily foot hygiene to prevent infections.
  • Avoid repeated toe injuries and protect feet during sports.
  • Use protective footwear if working in hazardous environments.
  • If diabetic, regularly check feet to prevent complications.

Treatment Options

Home Treatment

  • Soak feet in warm salt water 2–3 times daily.
  • Keep feet clean and dry.
  • Wear open or wide-toe footwear to reduce pressure.
  • Apply antiseptic cream to prevent infection.

Medical Treatment

  • Partial Nail Removal: Removal of part of the toenail if painful/infected.
  • Total Nail Removal: In severe or chronic cases, full toenail removal may be done.
  • Antibiotics: Used if infection is present.
  • Cauterization: Helps prevent regrowth of the problematic part of the nail.

Post-Treatment Care

  • Keep the toe elevated and dry initially.
  • Avoid tight shoes until healing.
  • Follow proper wound care instructions given by the doctor.

Ancient Treatments

Historically, ingrown toenails were treated using herbal soaks, poultices, and natural antiseptics such as honey or turmeric. While these methods may have offered minor relief, modern surgical and medical interventions are far more effective and safer.

Natural Remedies for Ingrown Toenails

Natural remedies may help in mild discomfort but cannot replace medical treatment for infected or chronic cases.

  • Warm salt water foot soaks to reduce swelling and discomfort.
  • Apple cider vinegar soaks (antibacterial effect).
  • Applying tea tree oil diluted in a carrier oil for infection control.
  • Turmeric paste as an anti-inflammatory application.
  • Wear cotton socks to keep the feet moisture-free.

⚠️ Severe ingrown toenails require medical care and delay may cause deep infection.
