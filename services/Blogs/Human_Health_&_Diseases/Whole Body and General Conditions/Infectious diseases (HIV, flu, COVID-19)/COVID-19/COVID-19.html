COVID-19 | Coronavirus (SARS-CoV-2) Infection

COVID-19 is a contagious respiratory illness caused by SARS-CoV-2, a novel coronavirus first identified in late 2019. The disease varies from mild to severe, with symptoms affecting the lungs, throat, and other organs. COVID-19 spreads via droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks, as well as from touching contaminated surfaces and then the face. Preventive measures and vaccines are key to controlling the pandemic.

Symptoms

COVID-19 symptoms vary but commonly include:

  • Fever, chills, and body aches
  • Dry cough and sore throat
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Fatigue and headache
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Diarrhea, nausea or vomiting
  • Muscle pain or joint aches
  • Chest discomfort in severe cases
  • Skin rashes or discoloration of fingers/toes (less common)

Causes

COVID-19 is caused by the novel coronavirus, SARS-CoV-2. The infection spreads rapidly in communities through:

  • Respiratory droplets released when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or speaks
  • Contact with contaminated surfaces (fomites)
  • Close contact in crowded or poorly ventilated spaces
  • Direct person-to-person contact (handshakes, hugs)
  • Aerosol transmission in enclosed environments

Children

  • Usually experience milder symptoms: fever, cough, mild respiratory symptoms
  • Some may develop multisystem inflammatory syndrome (MIS-C)
  • Transmission often occurs at schools or homes

Adults

  • Higher risk for moderate to severe symptoms, especially with comorbidities
  • Hospitalization required in severe or complicated cases
  • Reinfection and long COVID (persistent symptoms) possible

Aged People

  • Highest risk for severe illness, ICU admission, or death
  • Common complications: pneumonia, ARDS, blood clots, organ failure
  • Vaccine and booster protection strongly emphasized

Precautions | Prevention

  • Get vaccinated and keep up with booster doses
  • Wear masks in crowded or indoor settings
  • Maintain at least 1 meter social distance
  • Wash hands frequently or use sanitizer
  • Disinfect commonly touched surfaces
  • Avoid touching face, nose, or mouth unnecessarily
  • Monitor symptoms and self-isolate if unwell
  • Ventilate indoor spaces well
  • Stay home if exposed or symptomatic

Treatment Options

Home Isolation & Supportive Care

  • Rest, nutritious food, and plenty of fluids
  • Paracetamol for fever or pain
  • Pulse oximeter to monitor oxygen levels
  • Isolation from others to prevent spread

Medical Treatment

  • Antiviral medicines (like Remdesivir) for moderate-severe cases
  • Oxygen therapy if saturation drops below 94%
  • Steroid medications for inflammation in severe cases
  • Anticoagulants to reduce blood clot risks
  • Hospitalization for critical cases with respiratory distress, pneumonia, or multi-organ impact

Post-COVID Recovery

  • Gradual increase in physical activity
  • Breathing exercises and rehabilitation
  • Monitor for long COVID symptoms

Ancient Treatments

Before the discovery of the coronavirus, viral fevers were managed using herbal remedies, warm broths, inhaling steam with medicinal herbs, and yoga for respiratory health. Practices like drinking turmeric or ginger milk, tulsi, and pepper infusions were common for fever support but did not address viral infections specifically.

Natural Remedies for COVID-19

Supportive natural remedies can aid recovery but do not replace medical care or vaccines.

Diet and Lifestyle

  • Warm fluids: ginger tea, turmeric milk, herbal broths
  • Steam inhalation for nasal relief
  • Balanced diet with fresh fruits, vegetables, and proteins
  • Honey and lemon with warm water for sore throat
  • Practice regular breathing exercises (pranayama)
  • Take adequate rest and limit strenuous activities
  • Maintain hydration — at least 2–3 liters of water per day

Immunity Boosters

  • Vitamin C, zinc, and vitamin D supplements if deficient
  • Consume turmeric, garlic, and tulsi for natural support

Note: Natural remedies support general wellness but do not cure COVID-19. Seek medical help if symptoms worsen.
