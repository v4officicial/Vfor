Influenza (Flu) | Viral Respiratory Infection

Influenza, commonly known as the flu, is a viral respiratory infection caused by influenza viruses (Types A, B, and C). It spreads through airborne droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. The flu affects the nose, throat, and lungs, leading to fever, cough, sore throat, and fatigue. Severe cases can cause pneumonia and other complications, especially in children, older adults, and those with weak immunity.

Symptoms

Flu symptoms often appear suddenly and may vary in intensity. Common signs include:

  • Sudden high fever (100–104°F or 38–40°C)
  • Dry cough and sore throat
  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Muscle and body aches
  • Chills and sweating
  • Headache and fatigue
  • Loss of appetite
  • Weakness and dizziness
  • Occasional vomiting or diarrhea (especially in children)
  • Sleepiness or irritability

Causes

The flu is caused by influenza viruses that mutate frequently, resulting in seasonal outbreaks. It spreads mainly through airborne droplets and contact with contaminated surfaces.

  • Infection by influenza viruses Type A, B, or C
  • Transmission via coughs, sneezes, or talking
  • Touching contaminated objects and then eyes, nose, or mouth
  • Weakened immune system
  • Cold and dry weather aiding virus survival
  • Close contact in crowded or poorly ventilated areas
  • Lack of vaccination and poor hygiene practices

Children

  • Higher susceptibility due to developing immunity
  • Symptoms include fever, cough, sore throat, and upset stomach
  • Complications can include ear infections or pneumonia

Adults

  • Flu often causes severe fatigue, body aches, and dehydration
  • May worsen chronic conditions like asthma or diabetes

Aged People

  • High risk of complications like pneumonia or heart problems
  • Vaccination is strongly recommended annually
  • Symptoms may appear milder but last longer

Precautions | Prevention

  • Get an annual flu vaccine
  • Wash hands frequently with soap or sanitizer
  • Avoid touching eyes, nose, and mouth frequently
  • Maintain physical distance from infected individuals
  • Disinfect commonly used objects and surfaces
  • Use tissues or elbows while sneezing or coughing
  • Avoid overcrowded areas during flu season
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamin C and zinc
  • Wear masks in public if symptoms persist

Treatment Options

Antiviral Medications

  • Oseltamivir (Tamiflu), Zanamivir, or Baloxavir help reduce symptom severity and duration
  • Must be taken within 48 hours of symptom onset
  • Used for high-risk or severe cases

Symptomatic Treatment

  • Paracetamol or ibuprofen for fever and pain relief
  • Steam inhalation for nasal congestion
  • Cough syrups and throat lozenges for sore throat
  • Plenty of fluids and rest

Severe Cases

  • Hospitalization may be required for dehydration or pneumonia
  • Oxygen support and IV fluids in critical cases

Ancient Treatments

In ancient medicine, herbal mixtures of tulsi, ginger, and black pepper were used to reduce fever and clear phlegm. Ayurvedic treatments emphasized boosting immunity using herbs like ashwagandha, giloy, and amla. Chinese medicine used herbal teas to balance body energy and clear respiratory infections.

Natural Remedies for Flu

Natural remedies can ease flu symptoms and accelerate recovery alongside medical treatment.

Home and Dietary Remedies

  • Drink warm fluids like ginger tea, soup, and honey water
  • Consume citrus fruits for vitamin C boost
  • Use steam inhalation with eucalyptus oil
  • Honey and turmeric milk relieve throat irritation
  • Gargle with warm salt water twice daily

Immunity Boosters

  • Turmeric, garlic, and tulsi support natural defense
  • Include probiotics like yogurt in your diet
  • Drink sufficient fluids to prevent dehydration

Note: Natural remedies complement medical care but should not replace antiviral medications in severe flu cases.
