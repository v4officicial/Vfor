Typhoid | Enteric Fever Caused by Salmonella Typhi

Typhoid fever is a serious bacterial infection caused by *Salmonella Typhi*. It is transmitted through contaminated food or water and spread from person to person via the fecal-oral route. Typhoid leads to prolonged high fever, abdominal pain, and severe weakness. Without treatment, it can cause intestinal bleeding, perforation, and even death.

Symptoms

Typhoid develops gradually after an incubation period of 6–30 days. Major symptoms include:

  • Prolonged high fever (up to 104°F or 40°C)
  • Headache and muscle pain
  • Loss of appetite
  • Weakness and fatigue
  • Stomach pain, diarrhea, or constipation
  • Rose-colored spots on the chest and abdomen
  • Cough and mild respiratory symptoms
  • Swollen liver and spleen
  • Delirium or confusion in severe cases
  • Dry tongue and coated mouth
  • Body aches and chills

Causes

Typhoid fever is caused by the bacteria *Salmonella enterica* serotype Typhi. The infection spreads through ingestion of contaminated food or water. Poor sanitation and hygiene are major contributors.

  • Consumption of water or food contaminated with feces or urine of infected individuals
  • Street food or unwashed fruits and vegetables
  • Poor hand hygiene before eating or cooking
  • Contact with chronic carriers of Salmonella Typhi
  • Inadequate sewage disposal and untreated water supply
  • Living or traveling in endemic regions (South Asia, Africa, Latin America)

Children

  • Common due to weak immune systems and poor sanitation awareness
  • Symptoms include fever, irritability, diarrhea, and poor appetite
  • Severe dehydration can occur quickly in children

Adults

  • More likely acquired through contaminated food or travel to endemic regions
  • Excessive fatigue, high-grade fever, and abdominal discomfort are common
  • Relapse possible if antibiotics are not completed

Aged People

  • Typhoid can cause severe complications like intestinal bleeding or pneumonia
  • Weaker immunity leads to slower recovery
  • Frequent dehydration and confusion symptoms

Precautions | Prevention

  • Always drink boiled or filtered water
  • Avoid raw or uncooked foods and street food
  • Wash hands before eating or cooking
  • Peel fruits yourself to avoid contamination
  • Get vaccinated against Typhoid fever before traveling to risk areas
  • Maintain proper sanitation and personal hygiene
  • Avoid close contact with infected individuals
  • Disinfect food preparation areas
  • Use clean toilets and ensure safe food handling

Treatment Options

Antibiotic Therapy

  • Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin, or Ceftriaxone are the most commonly used antibiotics.
  • Treatment duration: 7–14 days depending on severity.
  • Antibiotic resistance is increasing, so sensitivity testing is essential.

Supportive Care

  • Hydration through oral rehydration solutions or IV fluids
  • Paracetamol for fever management
  • Nutritious light diet — soups, boiled rice, and fruits
  • Complete bed rest to support recovery

Hospitalization

  • Required for severe or resistant cases
  • Intestinal perforation or bleeding needs surgical intervention
  • Continuous monitoring for complications

Ancient Treatments

Historically, Typhoid was managed through herbal methods such as neem water, basil leaves, and turmeric milk. However, without antibiotics, mortality rates were high. Ayurvedic traditions used detoxification through herbs like *Guduchi* (Tinospora cordifolia) and *Tulsi*, believed to support immunity and reduce fever.

Natural Remedies for Typhoid

Typhoid requires prompt antibiotic treatment, but natural remedies can support recovery and strengthen immunity.

Dietary Support

  • Consume soft foods like boiled rice, porridge, and soups
  • Drink coconut water, lime juice, and herbal teas for hydration
  • Eat probiotic-rich foods like yogurt to restore gut health
  • Avoid spicy, oily, and processed foods

Herbal Remedies

  • Basil (Tulsi) tea reduces fever and boosts immunity
  • Fenugreek water helps manage digestive issues
  • Garlic has natural antibacterial properties
  • Clove and black pepper decoction for relief in sore throat

Hydration & Rest

  • Drink at least 3–4 liters of safe water per day
  • Take adequate rest to recover strength

Note: Natural remedies should only be used alongside prescribed medical treatment, not as a substitute.
