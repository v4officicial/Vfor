Obesity | Overweight Condition

Obesity is a chronic medical condition characterized by excessive body fat accumulation that poses health risks. It is commonly measured using Body Mass Index (BMI), where a BMI of 30 or higher is considered obese. Obesity increases the risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, stroke, certain cancers, and joint problems. Lifestyle choices, genetics, and metabolic factors all contribute to obesity.

Symptoms

  • Excessive weight gain with high body fat percentage
  • Difficulty in breathing and shortness of breath
  • Snoring or sleep apnea (breathing issues during sleep)
  • Joint pain and reduced mobility
  • Excessive sweating
  • Skin fold irritation and rashes
  • Tiredness or fatigue easily
  • Low self-esteem or body image concerns
  • Accumulation of fat around abdomen, hips, and thighs
  • High blood pressure and abnormal cholesterol
  • Development of insulin resistance and type 2 diabetes symptoms

Causes

Obesity is usually a result of multiple factors involving lifestyle, genetics, and environment. Common causes include:

  • Imbalance: consuming more calories than body burns
  • Sedentary lifestyle with little or no physical activity
  • Unhealthy diet high in fat, sugar, and processed foods
  • Genetics and family history
  • Hormonal disorders like hypothyroidism, PCOS
  • Medications (steroids, antidepressants) leading to weight gain
  • Psychological factors: stress, depression, emotional eating
  • Lack of sleep and irregular lifestyle habits

Children

  • High calorie fast foods and sugary drinks
  • Increased screen time and reduced outdoor activities
  • Lack of family focus on nutrition and exercise

Adults

  • Prolonged sedentary job routines
  • Alcohol consumption and junk food intake
  • Stress eating and lack of exercise
  • Insulin resistance and metabolic disorders

Aged People

  • Slower metabolism with aging
  • Reduced mobility and less activity
  • Multiple medications increasing weight

Precautions | Prevention

  • Follow a balanced diet with fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein
  • Limit sugar, fried foods, and high-fat diets
  • Exercise at least 150 minutes per week (walking, cardio, strength workouts)
  • Ensure good sleep hygiene of 7–8 hours daily
  • Manage stress with yoga, meditation, or relaxation activities
  • Avoid frequent consumption of alcohol and sweetened beverages
  • Maintain regular health check-ups for diabetes and cholesterol

Treatment Options

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Reduced-calorie diet with balanced nutrition
  • Regular exercise and physical activity
  • Behavioral therapy for eating habits

Medical Treatments

  • Prescription medicines for weight management
  • Treatment of underlying hormonal conditions

Surgical Options

  • Bariatric Surgery: Gastric bypass, sleeve gastrectomy, gastric banding
  • Reserved for people with BMI ≥40, or ≥35 with serious medical issues
⚠️ Untreated obesity can lead to serious chronic diseases like diabetes, heart attack, stroke, liver disease, and cancers.

Ancient Treatments

In ancient medicine, obesity was linked to imbalanced lifestyle and diet. Ayurveda suggested detox therapies, herbs like Triphala and Guggul, yoga and pranayama. Traditional Chinese Medicine used acupuncture and green tea. While partially effective for weight management, modern medicine provides more structured therapies.

Natural Remedies for Obesity

Natural methods can help manage and reduce excess weight effectively along with lifestyle changes:

  • Drinking warm water with lemon and honey in morning
  • Green tea for metabolism boost
  • High-fiber foods like oats, flaxseed, psyllium husk
  • Apple cider vinegar support with meals
  • Daily brisk walking and yoga
  • Herbal support: Garcinia cambogia, Triphala, Guggul

⚠️ Natural remedies should be coupled with diet and exercise. Severe obesity requires professional supervision.
