Lung Cancer | Lung Carcinoma

Lung cancer is a malignant tumor that starts in the lungs, caused by uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells. It is one of the most common and serious cancers worldwide. There are two main types: non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) and small cell lung cancer (SCLC), each requiring different treatment approaches. Early diagnosis improves chances of successful treatment.

Symptoms

Lung cancer symptoms often appear in advanced stages but may include:

  • A persistent cough that does not go away or worsens
  • Coughing up blood, even in small amounts
  • Chest pain that is often worse with deep breathing, coughing, or laughing
  • Shortness of breath and wheezing
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue and general weakness
  • Hoarseness or voice changes
  • Frequent respiratory infections such as pneumonia or bronchitis
  • Bone pain or swelling from metastases
  • Swelling of the face or neck

Causes

Lung cancer is primarily caused by damage to lung cells, mainly due to exposure to carcinogens. Common causes include:

  • Tobacco smoking, the leading cause globally
  • Secondhand smoke exposure
  • Exposure to radon gas in homes or workplaces
  • Occupational exposure to asbestos, arsenic, diesel exhaust, and other chemicals
  • Air pollution
  • Genetic mutations and family history
  • Previous radiation therapy to the chest

Children

  • Lung cancer is very rare in children but can occur
  • Caused mainly by genetic factors or exposure to carcinogens in rare cases
  • Symptoms and treatment are similar but tailored for pediatric patients

Adults

  • Most common in adults over 50 years
  • Smoking history is the predominant risk factor
  • Diagnosis often delayed due to nonspecific early symptoms

Older Adults

  • Lung cancer incidence rises with age
  • Older adults may have multiple health problems complicating treatment
  • Early detection through screening (low-dose CT scans) is recommended for high-risk individuals

Prevention & Precautions

  • Avoid smoking and tobacco products
  • Reduce exposure to secondhand smoke
  • Test homes for radon and mitigate if levels are high
  • Wear protective gear if working with asbestos or chemicals
  • Reduce air pollution exposure when possible
  • Maintain a healthy diet rich in fruits and vegetables
  • Regular health screening if you are at high risk
  • Avoid excessive exposure to radiation
  • Exercise regularly to boost overall health

Treatment Options

Surgery

  • Used mainly for early-stage non-small cell lung cancer
  • Lobectomy, segmentectomy, wedge resection, or pneumonectomy options
  • Goal is complete tumor removal

Chemotherapy

  • Systemic treatment to kill cancer cells
  • Used before or after surgery or as primary treatment in advanced stages
  • Often combined with other treatments

Radiation Therapy

  • Targeted radiation to destroy cancer cells
  • Used in patients not eligible for surgery or for symptom control
  • Includes stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT) for precise targeting

Targeted Therapy & Immunotherapy

  • Drugs targeting specific genetic mutations (EGFR, ALK, ROS1) or pathways
  • Immune checkpoint inhibitors help the immune system attack cancer cells
  • Important options for advanced or metastatic cases

Management of Symptoms and Complications

  • Palliative care to manage pain, breathing difficulties, and other symptoms
  • Procedures to relieve airway obstructions or fluid buildup around lungs

Comparison: Surgery vs. Systemic Therapy

Treatment Advantages Limitations
Surgery - Curative potential in early-stage cancers
- Removes entire tumor if accessible
- Better long-term survival for localized tumors 		- Invasive procedure with surgical risks
- Not suitable for advanced or metastatic disease
- Requires adequate lung function
Chemotherapy/Targeted/Immunotherapy - Applicable in advanced stages
- Can shrink tumors and prolong life
- Less invasive, systemic treatment 		- Side effects may include nausea, fatigue, immune reactions
- Not curative for all patients
- Requires ongoing treatment and monitoring
⚠️ Early diagnosis and treatment can improve survival. Persistent cough, blood in sputum, or chest pain should prompt medical attention.

Ancient Treatments

Historically, lung cancer was not well understood and treatments were limited to herbal remedies and symptom relief. Smoking was not recognized as a risk factor. Modern surgical, medical, and radiation techniques have vastly improved outcomes.

Natural & Lifestyle Support

Natural support complements medical treatment and promotes general lung health but is not a cure:

Diet and Lifestyle

  • A diet rich in antioxidants from fruits and vegetables
  • Maintaining hydration and avoiding processed foods
  • Breathing exercises and pulmonary rehabilitation
  • Reducing exposure to pollutants and irritants
  • Stress reduction and moderate physical activity

Herbal Support

  • Herbs like turmeric and ginger may reduce inflammation
  • Green tea antioxidants may provide protective effects
  • Consult doctors before using supplements to avoid interactions

Important Note

Natural remedies should never replace standard cancer treatments. Always follow medical advice.
