Anemia | Low Hemoglobin Condition

Anemia is a condition in which the body lacks enough healthy red blood cells or hemoglobin to carry adequate oxygen to tissues. This leads to fatigue, weakness, pale skin, and shortness of breath. Common types include iron deficiency anemia, vitamin B12 deficiency anemia, folate deficiency anemia, and anemia caused by chronic diseases or genetic disorders like sickle cell anemia.

Symptoms

Signs and symptoms of anemia vary depending on severity but commonly include:

  • Persistent fatigue or weakness
  • Pale or yellowish skin
  • Shortness of breath during exertion
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Irregular or rapid heartbeat
  • Chest pain in severe cases
  • Brittle nails or hair loss
  • Craving for non-food items like ice or clay (pica)
  • Poor concentration and headaches

Causes

  • Iron deficiency due to inadequate diet or chronic blood loss
  • Vitamin B12 or folate deficiency affecting red blood cell production
  • Chronic diseases (kidney disease, rheumatoid arthritis, cancer)
  • Genetic conditions (sickle cell anemia, thalassemia)
  • Bone marrow disorders or aplastic anemia
  • Heavy menstrual bleeding in women
  • Pregnancy without proper iron supplementation

Children

  • Poor nutritional intake or iron-deficient diet
  • Rapid growth increasing iron and vitamin needs
  • Parasitic infections or intestinal worms causing blood loss

Adults

  • Chronic illnesses, poor diet, and blood loss from ulcers or menstruation
  • Alcohol abuse causing malabsorption of nutrients
  • Poor iron or vitamin B12 absorption due to stomach disorders

Aged People

  • Decreased nutrient absorption due to aging gut
  • Chronic diseases like kidney failure affecting red blood cell production
  • Poor appetite and inadequate diet

Precautions | Preventions

  • Include iron-rich foods such as spinach, liver, lentils, and beans.
  • Consume vitamin C-rich fruits to enhance iron absorption.
  • Reduce tea or coffee during meals to avoid inhibiting iron absorption.
  • Maintain proper hygiene to prevent parasite-related anemia.
  • Get regular health checkups and hemoglobin tests.
  • Pregnant women should take prescribed iron and folic acid supplements.

Treatment Options

  • Iron supplements to restore iron levels in iron deficiency anemia.
  • Vitamin B12 and folate injections or tablets for nutritional deficiencies.
  • Blood transfusions for severe anemia or chronic conditions.
  • Erythropoietin injections to stimulate red blood cell production in kidney-related anemia.
  • Treat underlying cause such as infection, chronic disease, or bleeding source.

Ancient Treatments

In Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine, anemia was treated using herbs such as ashwagandha, amla, ginseng, and jaggery-based tonics to enhance blood formation. They emphasized a balanced diet, liver tonics, and energy restoration herbs to address weakness.

Natural Remedies for Anemia

  • Drink beetroot and carrot juice daily to boost hemoglobin.
  • Eat jaggery with sesame seeds for natural iron intake.
  • Consume pomegranate, dates, and raisins regularly.
  • Practice mild yoga and breathing exercises to improve oxygen levels.

⚠️ Natural remedies complement medical treatment but should not replace prescribed supplements or transfusions.
