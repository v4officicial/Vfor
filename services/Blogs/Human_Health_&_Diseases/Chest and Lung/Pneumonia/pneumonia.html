Pneumonia | Lung Infection

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs, which may fill with fluid or pus. It can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi, leading to cough, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing. Pneumonia ranges from mild to life-threatening, being especially serious for infants, older adults, and people with weakened immune systems.

Symptoms

The symptoms of pneumonia vary by cause, age, and severity. Common symptoms include:

  • Persistent cough producing phlegm
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fever, sweating, and chills
  • Chest pain when breathing or coughing
  • Rapid or shallow breathing
  • Confusion in older adults
  • Loss of appetite and fatigue
  • In severe cases, bluish lips and nails due to low oxygen

Causes

Pneumonia is caused by various infectious agents and sometimes through inhalation of irritants. Main causes include:

  • Bacterial infection (most commonly Streptococcus pneumoniae)
  • Viral infections such as influenza, RSV, or COVID-19
  • Fungal infections in people with weak immunity
  • Aspiration pneumonia from inhaling food, liquids or vomit
  • Hospital-acquired pneumonia from long hospital stays
  • Weak immune system due to chronic illnesses

Children

  • Viral causes such as RSV and influenza
  • Bacterial pneumonia spreading from school/community
  • Malnutrition increasing infection risk

Adults

  • Bacterial pneumonia from Streptococcus pneumoniae
  • Complication of seasonal flu or COVID-19
  • Smoking damaging lung defenses
  • Workplace exposure to irritants

Aged People

  • Weakened immunity with age
  • Pre-existing chronic lung or heart diseases
  • Hospital or ventilator acquired pneumonia
  • Difficulty swallowing increasing aspiration risks

Precautions | Preventions

  • Get vaccinated against flu, pneumococcus, and COVID-19.
  • Wash hands regularly to prevent infections.
  • Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke exposure.
  • Strengthen immunity with good nutrition and exercise.
  • Avoid close contact with people who have respiratory infections.
  • Maintain good hygiene in medical facilities to reduce hospital-acquired pneumonia.

Treatment Options

Medical Approaches

  • Antibiotics for bacterial pneumonia
  • Antiviral drugs for viral pneumonia (like influenza or COVID-19)
  • Antifungal medications for fungal pneumonia
  • Fever reducers and pain medications

Supportive Care

  • Rest and hydration
  • Oxygen therapy in severe cases
  • Hospital care for patients with complications
⚠️ Severe pneumonia can be life-threatening, particularly in infants, elderly, and those with chronic diseases. Early medical intervention is essential.

Ancient Treatments

In traditional medicine systems, lung infections like pneumonia were treated with herbal decoctions containing tulsi, ginger, turmeric, mulethi (licorice root), and pepper. Steam inhalation and warm salted water gargling were used to ease congestion and sore throat. Though supportive, these methods were not curative.

Natural Remedies for Pneumonia

Natural remedies support medical treatment by boosting immunity and easing symptoms, but they should not replace prescribed medicines.

Diet and Lifestyle Approaches

  • Warm soups, herbal teas, and easily digestible foods
  • Hydration to thin mucus secretions
  • Rest for faster recovery

Herbal and Home Remedies

  • Ginger or turmeric tea for anti-inflammatory relief
  • Honey for soothing cough
  • Tulsi decoction for respiratory support
  • Garlic for its antibacterial properties

Additional Measures

  • Steam inhalation with eucalyptus oil
  • Warm compress on chest to reduce discomfort
  • Breathing exercises post-recovery to strengthen lungs

⚠️ Natural remedies are supportive and must be combined with proper medical treatment for pneumonia.
