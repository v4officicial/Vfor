Lung Cancer | Respiratory Cancer

Lung cancer is one of the most common and serious types of cancer, originating in the lungs. It is broadly classified into small cell lung cancer (SCLC) and non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). It often develops in people with a history of smoking, though non-smokers can also develop it due to genetic and environmental factors.

Symptoms

In early stages, lung cancer may not present noticeable symptoms. As it advances, common symptoms include:

  • Persistent cough that doesn’t go away
  • Coughing up blood (hemoptysis)
  • Chest pain that worsens with deep breathing, coughing, or laughing
  • Shortness of breath
  • Wheezing and hoarseness
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Bone pain (if cancer spreads)
  • Headaches (due to metastasis)
  • Recurrent chest infections (bronchitis/pneumonia)

Causes

Lung cancer develops when cells in the lungs undergo mutations leading to uncontrolled growth. Major causes and risk factors include:

  • Smoking tobacco (cigarettes, cigars, pipes)
  • Secondhand smoke exposure
  • Long-term exposure to carcinogens like asbestos, radon, or arsenic
  • Air pollution and industrial fumes
  • Family history of lung cancer
  • Weakened immunity or past radiation therapy to the chest

In Younger People

  • Rare genetic mutation-driven cancers
  • Exposure to passive smoking
  • Environmental carcinogen exposure

Adults

  • Active or long-term smoking
  • Workplace exposure to dust, asbestos, or chemicals
  • High pollution areas

Aged People

  • Cumulative exposure to carcinogens over lifetime
  • Weakened DNA repair mechanisms with age
  • More prevalent in smokers above 60

Precautions | Preventions

  • Avoid smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke.
  • Test homes and buildings for radon exposure.
  • Reduce occupational exposure to asbestos and chemicals.
  • Maintain a healthy diet rich in fruits and vegetables.
  • Exercise regularly to boost immunity.
  • Regular screening for high-risk individuals (especially long-term smokers).

Treatment Options

Medical and Surgical Approaches

  • Surgical removal of lung tumors (lobectomy, pneumonectomy)
  • Radiation therapy for localized tumors
  • Chemotherapy (systemic drug treatment)
  • Targeted therapy based on gene mutations
  • Immunotherapy for advanced cases

For Advanced Stages

  • Palliative care to manage symptoms
  • Treatment for bone pain and metastasis
  • Oxygen therapy for breathing difficulty
⚠️ Lung cancer detected at an early stage has better survival rates. Screening and preventive measures are crucial for high-risk people.

Ancient Treatments

In ancient medicine systems like Ayurveda, lung-related cancers were not well defined, but breathing difficulties and tumor-like symptoms were managed with herbs such as turmeric, tulsi, and licorice. Detox therapies, dietary modifications, and spiritual practices were recommended. However, these approaches were supportive and not curative.

Natural Remedies for Lung Cancer

Natural remedies cannot cure lung cancer but may improve well-being and support recovery along with conventional therapy.

Diet and Lifestyle Approaches

  • Eat antioxidant-rich foods such as turmeric, green tea, and leafy vegetables.
  • Maintain lung health with deep-breathing exercises.
  • Avoid processed foods and alcohol.

Herbal and Home Remedies

  • Turmeric and ginger for anti-inflammatory support
  • Green tea polyphenols for immune support
  • Garlic to strengthen immunity
  • Tulsi (holy basil) for respiratory health

Additional Measures

  • Maintain clean indoor air with air purifiers.
  • Practice pranayama for lung strengthening.
  • Engage in light exercise under medical guidance.

⚠️ Natural remedies are complementary and should never replace evidence-based medical treatment for lung cancer.
