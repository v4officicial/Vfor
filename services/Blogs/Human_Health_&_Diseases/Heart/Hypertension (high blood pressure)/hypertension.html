Hypertension | High Blood Pressure

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, occurs when the force of blood against artery walls remains consistently too high. It is a major risk factor for heart disease, stroke, kidney failure, and other complications. Often called the "silent killer," hypertension may not cause symptoms for years but can cause serious damage if untreated.

Symptoms

Hypertension usually has no obvious symptoms, but advanced or severe cases may present with:

  • Persistent headaches
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Blurred vision
  • Chest pain or tightness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue or confusion
  • Nosebleeds (in rare cases)
  • Irregular heartbeat

Causes

Hypertension can result from genetic, lifestyle, and underlying medical factors. Major causes include:

  • Unhealthy lifestyle, poor diet with high salt intake
  • Obesity or being overweight
  • Physical inactivity
  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Endocrine disorders (thyroid, diabetes)
  • Excessive alcohol consumption or smoking
  • Stress and anxiety
  • Family history of hypertension

Children

  • Congenital heart or kidney problems
  • Obesity due to poor diet and inactivity
  • Secondary hypertension from medical conditions
  • Genetic predisposition

Adults

  • High stress lifestyle
  • Obesity and poor dietary choices
  • Sedentary habits
  • Regular alcohol consumption and smoking

Aged People

  • Age-related stiffening of arteries
  • Long-standing hypertension complications
  • Multiple chronic conditions
  • Polypharmacy (multiple drugs with side effects)

Precautions | Preventions

  • Follow a low-salt, balanced diet such as the DASH diet.
  • Maintain a healthy weight with regular physical activity.
  • Quit smoking and avoid excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Limit caffeine and processed foods.
  • Manage stress with yoga, breathing, or meditation.
  • Get 7-8 hours of regular sleep.
  • Regularly monitor blood pressure at home.

Treatment Options

Lifestyle and Preventive Care

  • Low-salt diet rich in vegetables, fruits, and whole grains.
  • Regular aerobic activity like brisk walking, jogging, cycling.
  • Weight loss for overweight individuals.
  • Stress management practices such as yoga or deep breathing.

Medication Support

  • Diuretics
  • ACE inhibitors
  • Beta-blockers
  • Calcium channel blockers
  • ARBs (angiotensin receptor blockers)

For Severe or Resistant Cases

  • Combination drug therapies
  • Ongoing monitoring of target organ damage (kidney, heart, eyes)
  • Treatment of secondary hypertension causes
⚠️ Hypertension requires regular monitoring. Untreated hypertension can result in stroke, heart attack, kidney damage, and other life-threatening complications.

Ancient Treatments

Ancient medicine for hypertension focused on lifestyle and herbal remedies. Ayurveda recommended herbs like Ashwagandha, Sarpagandha, and Arjuna bark to manage blood pressure and stress. Traditional Chinese Medicine used acupuncture, qigong, and herbal mixtures to balance internal energy and reduce hypertension risk.

Natural Remedies for Hypertension

Natural remedies help control early or mild hypertension when combined with lifestyle management.

Diet and Lifestyle Approaches

  • Eat potassium-rich foods (bananas, spinach, oranges).
  • Include omega-3 fatty acids from flaxseeds, walnuts, and fish.
  • Use garlic and turmeric for heart protection.
  • Practice meditation, yoga, and pranayama daily.

Herbal and Food Remedies

  • Sarpagandha (Rauwolfia serpentina) to reduce elevated pressure.
  • Ashwagandha to manage stress-induced hypertension.
  • Green tea for antioxidant and cardiovascular health.
  • Beetroot juice for lowering systolic blood pressure naturally.

Additional Measures

  • Walking in fresh air for 30 minutes daily.
  • Maintaining a consistent sleep routine.

⚠️ Natural remedies are supportive; medical treatment should never be replaced in cases of moderate to severe hypertension.
