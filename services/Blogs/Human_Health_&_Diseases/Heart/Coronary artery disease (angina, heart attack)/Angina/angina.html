Angina | Chest Pain

Angina is chest pain or discomfort caused by reduced blood flow to the heart muscle, often due to narrowing of the coronary arteries. While not a disease itself, it is a symptom of underlying heart disease, typically coronary artery disease (CAD). Angina requires medical attention as it may signal an increased risk of heart attack.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of angina include:

  • Chest pain or pressure, often described as squeezing or burning
  • Pain spreading to arms, neck, jaw, shoulder, or back
  • Shortness of breath
  • Sweating (cold sweat)
  • Nausea or indigestion-like discomfort
  • Dizziness or fatigue
  • Pain triggered by physical activity or stress (in stable angina)
  • Pain occurring suddenly or unpredictably (in unstable angina)

Causes

Angina is typically caused by reduced blood supply (ischemia) to heart muscles. Common causes include:

  • Coronary artery disease (plaque buildup)
  • Uncontrolled high blood pressure
  • Spasm of coronary arteries (Prinzmetal’s angina)
  • Clot formation in coronary artery
  • Anemia reducing oxygen supply to the heart
  • Severe aortic stenosis

Children

  • Rare in children, but may occur with congenital heart defects
  • Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy
  • Severe anemia or Kawasaki disease complications

Adults

  • Most common cause is coronary artery disease
  • Uncontrolled hypertension or diabetes
  • High cholesterol levels
  • Poor lifestyle habits (smoking, sedentary life)

Aged People

  • Age-related stiffening of arteries
  • More severe atherosclerosis and multiple comorbidities
  • Reduced tolerance to cardiac workload

Precautions | Preventions

  • Maintain a healthy weight and manage cholesterol levels.
  • Follow a balanced heart-healthy diet (low in salt, sugar, and saturated fats).
  • Exercise regularly under doctor supervision.
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol intake.
  • Manage stress effectively.
  • Regular cardiac checkups for those with risk factors.
  • Take medications as prescribed for diabetes, hypertension, or cholesterol.

Treatment Options

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Adopt a healthy diet (Mediterranean or DASH diet)
  • Exercise regularly but moderately
  • Weight management and stress reduction

Medication Support

  • Nitroglycerin to relieve chest pain
  • Beta-blockers and calcium channel blockers to reduce heart workload
  • Antiplatelet agents (like aspirin) to prevent blood clots
  • Statins to lower cholesterol and stabilize plaques

Medical and Surgical Procedures

  • Coronary angioplasty with stent placement
  • Coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery
⚠️ Any sudden or severe chest pain should be treated as a medical emergency, as it may indicate unstable angina or heart attack.

Ancient Treatments

Ancient remedies for chest pain included herbal preparations such as garlic, ginger, and hawthorn, believed to improve circulation. Ayurveda used arjuna bark for strengthening the heart, alongside yoga and breathing techniques to reduce stress and improve cardiac function.

Natural Remedies for Angina

Natural measures support general heart health but cannot replace standard medical therapy for angina.

Diet and Lifestyle Approaches

  • Follow a high-fiber diet with fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
  • Include foods rich in omega-3 fatty acids.
  • Reduce salt, sugar, and processed foods.

Herbal and Complementary Remedies

  • Garlic and turmeric may reduce inflammation.
  • Hawthorn extract traditionally used for heart support.
  • Green tea has antioxidant properties for heart health.

Additional Measures

  • Practice yoga and meditation to manage stress.
  • Avoid overexertion, maintain adequate rest.

⚠️ Natural remedies should be considered supportive. Always consult a doctor before combining alternative therapies with prescription medications.
