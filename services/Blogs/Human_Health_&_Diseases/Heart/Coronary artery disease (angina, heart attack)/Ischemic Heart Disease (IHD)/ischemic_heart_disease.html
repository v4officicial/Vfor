Ischemic Heart Disease (IHD) | Coronary Artery Disease

Ischemic Heart Disease (IHD), also known as Coronary Artery Disease (CAD), occurs when the coronary arteries that supply blood to the heart muscle become narrowed or blocked. This reduces blood and oxygen supply to the heart, leading to angina, heart attack, or chronic heart failure if not managed.

Symptoms

Symptoms of IHD may vary in severity and presentation. Common signs include:

  • Chest pain or pressure, often triggered by exertion or stress (angina)
  • Pain radiating to shoulder, arm, jaw, or back
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Irregular heartbeat or palpitations
  • Cold sweats and dizziness
  • Nausea or indigestion-like discomfort
  • Silent ischemia in some people (no obvious symptoms, especially in diabetics)

Causes

The major cause is atherosclerosis (plaque buildup in coronary arteries). Other contributing factors include:

  • High cholesterol and triglycerides
  • High blood pressure
  • Smoking and excessive alcohol use
  • Diabetes and insulin resistance
  • Obesity and lack of exercise
  • Family history of heart disease
  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Age-related arterial stiffening

Children

  • Very rare, but can occur with congenital coronary anomalies
  • Lipid metabolism disorders
  • Childhood obesity with multiple risk factors

Adults

  • Atherosclerosis-related blockage
  • Smoking, diabetes, and sedentary lifestyle
  • Obesity and hypertension
  • High-stress environments

Aged People

  • Long-standing uncontrolled hypertension and diabetes
  • Advanced arterial disease with multiple blockages
  • Increased vulnerability to angina and heart attacks

Precautions | Preventions

  • Adopt a balanced diet low in saturated fats, with high intake of fruits and vegetables.
  • Maintain healthy cholesterol, blood pressure, and blood sugar levels.
  • Exercise regularly for heart health (walking, jogging, swimming).
  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol consumption.
  • Maintain a healthy weight.
  • Regular medical check-ups if you are at risk.
  • Manage stress effectively with yoga, meditation, or hobbies.

Treatment Options

Lifestyle Management

  • Adopt a Mediterranean or DASH diet.
  • Exercise regularly within tolerance.
  • Weight management and smoking cessation.

Medication Support

  • Nitroglycerin for angina relief
  • Beta-blockers and calcium channel blockers
  • Antiplatelets (aspirin, clopidogrel) to prevent clot formation
  • Statins for cholesterol management
  • ACE inhibitors/ARBs for blood pressure and heart protection

Procedures and Surgery

  • Angioplasty with stent placement
  • Coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery
  • Enhanced external counterpulsation (EECP) in selected stable cases
⚠️ Early detection and treatment of IHD reduces risk of heart attacks, arrhythmias, and sudden cardiac death.

Ancient Treatments

Historically, IHD-like symptoms were treated with natural remedies such as garlic, hawthorn, and arjuna bark. Ayurveda emphasized yoga, pranayama, and herbal tonics to maintain cardiovascular strength, while Traditional Chinese Medicine used acupuncture and circulation-enhancing herbs.

Natural Remedies for IHD

Natural remedies help strengthen heart function and lower risk, but should complement medical treatment.

Diet and Lifestyle Approaches

  • Follow a Mediterranean-style diet rich in olive oil, nuts, and fish.
  • Engage in moderate regular exercise.
  • Reduce stress to lower cardiac strain.

Herbal and Food Remedies

  • Garlic, turmeric, and ginger can improve circulation and reduce inflammation.
  • Hawthorn berry supports heart function naturally.
  • Omega-3 fatty acids (fish oil, flaxseed) help lower cholesterol.
  • Arjuna bark is used in Ayurveda for strengthening the myocardium.

Additional Measures

  • Yoga, meditation, and pranayama for stress control.
  • Maintain adequate sleep and rest.

⚠️ Natural remedies are supportive and preventive. They cannot reverse blocked arteries and should be combined with medical care.
