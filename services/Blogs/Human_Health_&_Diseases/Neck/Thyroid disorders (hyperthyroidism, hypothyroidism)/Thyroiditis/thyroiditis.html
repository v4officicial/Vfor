Thyroiditis | Thyroid Inflammation

Thyroiditis refers to inflammation of the thyroid gland. It can be temporary or chronic, leading to overproduction (hyperthyroidism) or underproduction (hypothyroidism) of thyroid hormones. The most common types include Hashimoto’s thyroiditis, subacute thyroiditis, postpartum thyroiditis, and silent thyroiditis.

Symptoms

Symptoms depend on the cause and stage (early hyperthyroid vs later hypothyroid phase). They may include:

  • Pain or tenderness in the neck (common in subacute thyroiditis)
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Neck swelling or lump
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Unexplained weight changes
  • Palpitations and anxiety (hyperthyroid phase)
  • Cold intolerance and weight gain (hypothyroid phase)
  • Hair loss and dry skin
  • Depression and sluggishness

Causes

Thyroiditis has multiple causes, often involving autoimmune reactions, infections, or hormonal factors. Main causes include:

  • Hashimoto’s thyroiditis (autoimmune, most common)
  • Subacute thyroiditis (viral infection-related, painful)
  • Postpartum thyroiditis (occurs after pregnancy)
  • Silent thyroiditis (painless, autoimmune cause)
  • Radiation therapy or certain medications (e.g., amiodarone, interferon)
  • Bacterial infections (rare cause)

Children

  • Autoimmune thyroiditis (Hashimoto’s)
  • Congenital susceptibility or family history
  • Viral illnesses triggering subacute inflammation

Adults

  • Postpartum thyroiditis in women
  • Hashimoto’s thyroiditis in middle-aged adults
  • Drug-induced cases (lithium, interferon)

Aged People

  • Increased autoimmune thyroid disease prevalence
  • Subclinical thyroiditis often diagnosed during routine tests
  • Higher risk of permanent hypothyroidism after inflammation

Precautions | Preventions

  • Regular thyroid checkups if family history is present.
  • Avoid unnecessary radiation and certain high-risk medications unless prescribed.
  • Eat a balanced diet with sufficient iodine but avoid excess.
  • Manage stress with yoga or meditation.
  • Monitor postpartum women more closely for thyroid activity changes.

Treatment Options

Medical Management

  • Anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) or corticosteroids for painful thyroiditis
  • Beta-blockers to control symptoms in hyperthyroid phase
  • Levothyroxine (thyroid hormone replacement) for hypothyroid phase
  • Antibiotics if bacterial thyroiditis suspected

Lifestyle and Supportive Care

  • Plenty of rest and stress management
  • Monitor thyroid function (TSH, T4) regularly

For Severe or Chronic Cases

  • Permanent thyroid hormone replacement if hypothyroidism persists
  • Surgical removal (rare, when refractory to therapies)
⚠️ Thyroiditis may progress from hyperthyroid to hypothyroid stages. Regular monitoring and medical supervision are crucial.

Ancient Treatments

In ancient practice, thyroid inflammation signs were treated with anti-inflammatory herbs, warm oil applications, and detoxifying regimens. Ayurveda suggested herbs like Kanchanar, Guggulu, and Ashwagandha for hormonal and glandular balance.

Natural Remedies for Thyroiditis

Natural remedies can complement medical treatment but cannot replace it.

Diet and Lifestyle Approaches

  • Eat selenium-rich foods (Brazil nuts, sunflower seeds) to support thyroid function.
  • Avoid excessive iodine that can worsen thyroid inflammation.
  • Consume anti-inflammatory foods. (turmeric, green tea, vegetables)
  • Get sufficient rest and stress management practices.

Herbal Support

  • Ashwagandha for balancing energy and hormones.
  • Kanchanar Guggulu in Ayurveda for glandular swellings.
  • Licorice root for adrenal and thyroid support.

Additional Measures

  • Yoga poses like Sarvangasana for thyroid health.
  • Mindfulness and meditation for recovery support.

⚠️ Natural remedies are supportive. For prolonged hypothyroidism or hyperthyroid symptoms, clinical treatment remains essential.
