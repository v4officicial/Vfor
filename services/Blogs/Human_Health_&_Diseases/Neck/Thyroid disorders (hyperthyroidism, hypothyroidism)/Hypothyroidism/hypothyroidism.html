Hypothyroidism | Underactive Thyroid

Hypothyroidism occurs when the thyroid gland produces insufficient thyroid hormones, leading to a slowed metabolism. This can cause fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, and many systemic issues. It may develop gradually and requires long-term management.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of hypothyroidism include:

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Unexplained weight gain
  • Cold intolerance
  • Dry skin and brittle nails
  • Puffy face and swelling (edema)
  • Constipation
  • Depression and forgetfulness
  • Hoarseness of voice
  • Hair thinning or loss
  • Goiter (in some cases)
  • Slow heart rate and slowed reflexes

Causes

Hypothyroidism can be due to autoimmune, environmental, or medical factors. The most common causes include:

  • Hashimoto’s thyroiditis (autoimmune destruction)
  • Iodine deficiency (especially in certain regions)
  • Thyroid surgery or radioactive iodine treatment
  • Medications such as lithium or amiodarone
  • Congenital hypothyroidism (present at birth)
  • Pituitary or hypothalamic dysfunction (secondary hypothyroidism)

Children

  • Congenital hypothyroidism
  • Iodine deficiency impairing thyroid development
  • Delayed growth and intellectual development if untreated

Adults

  • Hashimoto’s thyroiditis (autoimmune)
  • Iatrogenic causes (medications, thyroidectomy)
  • Radiation therapy-induced thyroid damage

Aged People

  • Increased risk with age due to thyroid gland atrophy
  • Symptoms often mistaken for aging (fatigue, memory loss)
  • Greater risk of cardiovascular complications

Precautions | Preventions

  • Ensure adequate iodine intake in the diet (iodized salt).
  • Regular screening if you have a family history of thyroid issues.
  • Avoid unnecessary use of medications that affect thyroid function.
  • Routine thyroid function tests especially for older adults.
  • Follow prescribed thyroxine treatment regularly without skipping doses.
  • Maintain a healthy diet and regular exercise to reduce associated risks.

Treatment Options

Lifestyle and Dietary Changes

  • Eat a balanced diet with adequate protein and micronutrients.
  • Exercise regularly to maintain metabolism and weight.
  • Get adequate sleep for energy restoration.

Medication Support

  • Levothyroxine (synthetic thyroid hormone replacement therapy)
  • Regular monitoring of TSH and T4 levels for dose adjustment

For Severe Cases

  • High-dose thyroid hormone replacement if critically low levels
  • Hospitalization may be required for myxedema coma (rare but life-threatening)
⚠️ Hypothyroidism is usually lifelong and requires continuous medication. Do not stop treatment without consulting a doctor.

Ancient Treatments

In traditional medicine, hypothyroid-like symptoms were treated with warming foods, energy-restoring herbs, and massage therapies. Ayurveda suggested herbs like Ashwagandha, Guggulu, and licorice to support thyroid function, though modern hormone replacement is more accurate and reliable.

Natural Remedies for Hypothyroidism

Natural remedies work best alongside prescribed levothyroxine therapy.

Diet and Lifestyle Approaches

  • Consume iodine-rich foods (fish, dairy, iodized salt) unless restricted.
  • Avoid excessive consumption of goitrogenic foods like raw cabbage or soy.
  • Exercise daily to stabilize energy levels and control weight.

Herbal and Food Remedies

  • Ashwagandha to support thyroid function and energy.
  • Guggulu in Ayurveda to enhance thyroid hormone activity.
  • Selenium-rich foods (Brazil nuts, sunflower seeds) to aid thyroid hormone metabolism.

Additional Measures

  • Regular thyroid function monitoring.
  • Yoga practices like Sarvangasana to support endocrine health.

⚠️ Natural therapies may relieve mild symptoms but should never replace prescribed thyroid hormone therapy.
