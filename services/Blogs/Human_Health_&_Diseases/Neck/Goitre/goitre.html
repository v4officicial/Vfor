Goitre | Enlarged Thyroid

Goitre is an abnormal enlargement of the thyroid gland, located in the front of the neck below the Adam’s apple. It may be caused by iodine deficiency, autoimmune thyroid disorders, thyroid nodules, or other thyroid conditions. While sometimes painless, goitre can cause cosmetic concerns and symptoms related to compression or thyroid dysfunction.

Symptoms

Symptoms of goitre depend on size, cause, and thyroid gland function and may include:

  • Visible swelling or lump at the front of the neck
  • Feeling of tightness or pressure in the neck
  • Difficulty swallowing or breathing, especially lying down
  • Hoarseness or voice changes
  • Coughing or throat irritation
  • Symptoms of hyperthyroidism (weight loss, palpitations, tremors) if toxic goitre
  • Symptoms of hypothyroidism (fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance) if underactive thyroid

Causes

Goitre arises from a variety of causes affecting thyroid function or structure, including:

  • Iodine deficiency (worldwide most common cause)
  • Autoimmune diseases like Graves’ disease and Hashimoto’s thyroiditis
  • Thyroid nodules or cysts
  • Thyroid cancer (rare)
  • Pregnancy or hormonal changes
  • Thyroid inflammation (thyroiditis)

Types of Goitre

Diffuse Goitre

Diffuse goitre is a uniform enlargement of the entire thyroid gland without lumps. It is often seen in iodine deficiency and autoimmune thyroid diseases such as Graves' disease and Hashimoto’s thyroiditis.

Multinodular Goitre

This type has an enlarged thyroid with multiple nodules or lumps. It develops over years and may cause compressive symptoms. It can be toxic (overactive) or non-toxic (normal thyroid function).

Toxic Goitre

Toxic goitre refers to an enlarged thyroid gland producing excess thyroid hormones causing hyperthyroidism. Graves’ disease is a common cause. Symptoms include weight loss, heat intolerance, and palpitations.

Non-toxic Goitre

Non-toxic goitre is an enlarged thyroid without abnormal hormone production. Causes include iodine deficiency and multinodular goitre without hyperthyroidism.

Children

  • Iodine deficiency due to poor diet
  • Congenital hypothyroidism
  • Autoimmune thyroid disorders

Adults

  • Dietary iodine deficiency
  • Autoimmune thyroid disorders
  • Nodular goitre development
  • Pregnancy-related enlargement

Aged People

  • Long-term multinodular goitre
  • Postmenopausal changes in thyroid function
  • Autoimmune thyroiditis

Precautions | Preventions

  • Ensure adequate iodine intake through iodized salt and iodine-rich foods.
  • Regular thyroid function tests especially if family history or symptoms present.
  • Manage autoimmune thyroid conditions under physician guidance.
  • Avoid excessive iodine supplementation without medical advice.
  • Follow a balanced, nutritious diet for overall endocrine health.

Treatment Options

Lifestyle and Dietary Management

  • Correct iodine deficiency with diet or supplements as advised.
  • Regular monitoring of thyroid hormone levels.

Medication Support

  • Thyroid hormone replacement therapy for hypothyroid goitre
  • Anti-thyroid drugs for toxic goitre causing hyperthyroidism
  • Iodine supplements if deficiency confirmed

Surgical and Advanced Treatments

  • Partial or total thyroidectomy for large, symptomatic, or suspicious goitres
  • Radioactive iodine therapy for toxic nodular goitre
⚠️ Large goitres causing airway or swallowing difficulties should be evaluated for surgical removal.

Ancient Treatments

Traditional medicine used iodine-rich foods like seaweed for goitre prevention. Ayurveda recommended kanchanar guggulu and other herbal remedies for thyroid enlargement. Neck massages, dietary corrections, and yoga were also common supportive treatments.

Natural Remedies for Goitre

Natural remedies support thyroid health and may help prevent mild enlargement caused by nutritional deficiencies.

Dietary Approaches

  • Include iodized salt and iodine-rich foods like fish, dairy, and seaweed.
  • Brazil nuts for selenium support of thyroid function.
  • Limit intake of goitrogenic foods (excess raw cruciferous vegetables) if iodine deficient.

Herbal and Traditional Remedies

  • Kanchanar guggulu and ashwagandha for thyroid support (in Ayurveda).
  • Seaweed supplements under medical supervision.

Additional Measures

  • Stress management through yoga and meditation.
  • Regular exercise and adequate rest.

⚠️ Natural remedies should not replace medical evaluation for large or suspicious goitres.
