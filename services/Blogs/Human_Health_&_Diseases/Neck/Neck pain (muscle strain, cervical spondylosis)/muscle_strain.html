Muscle Strain | Pulled Muscle

Muscle strain, commonly called a pulled muscle, occurs when muscle fibers are overstretched or torn due to sudden force, overuse, or improper movement. It can affect any muscle in the body but is most common in the lower back, hamstrings, shoulders, and neck. Severity can range from mild overstretching to partial or complete muscle tears.

Symptoms

Symptoms of muscle strain vary based on the severity of injury. Common signs include:

  • Sudden pain during physical activity
  • Swelling and inflammation around the injured muscle
  • Muscle stiffness and weakness
  • Bruising or skin discoloration in severe strains
  • Limited range of motion or difficulty moving the muscle
  • Muscle spasms or cramping
  • A popping or tearing sensation at injury onset

Causes

Muscle strains usually occur due to overstretching or overloading the muscle beyond its capacity. Key causes include:

  • Improper warm-up before exercise
  • Sudden heavy lifting or overexertion
  • Overuse from repetitive motions
  • Poor posture and wrong movement techniques
  • Lack of flexibility and weak supporting muscles
  • Fatigue leading to improper body mechanics

Children & Teens

  • Sports injuries during play or training
  • Insufficient warm-up before intense activity
  • Sudden movements or falls

Adults

  • Strenuous gym exercises without preparation
  • Workplace injuries involving lifting
  • Poor posture from prolonged sitting

Elderly

  • Age-related muscle weakness and reduced flexibility
  • Minor movements causing strains due to fragile tissues
  • Falls or slips leading to severe pulling injuries

Precautions | Preventions

  • Always warm up properly before exercise or physical activity.
  • Include stretching exercises to improve flexibility.
  • Maintain strong core and supporting muscles.
  • Use correct techniques when lifting heavy objects.
  • Avoid sudden, uncontrolled movements.
  • Stay hydrated and maintain adequate nutrition for muscle health.
  • Wear supportive footwear during sports or running.

Treatment Options

Immediate First Aid (R.I.C.E.)

  • Rest the injured muscle by stopping physical activity.
  • Apply Ice packs to reduce inflammation.
  • Use Compression with bandages to minimize swelling.
  • Elevate the affected area to prevent blood pooling.

Medical Support

  • NSAIDs like ibuprofen for pain and inflammation
  • Muscle relaxants in severe spasms
  • Physical therapy for guided rehabilitation
  • In severe tears, surgical repair may be required

Recovery & Rehabilitation

  • Gradual stretching exercises after pain subsides
  • Strengthening exercises to prevent recurrence
  • Return to activity only after complete recovery
⚠️ Severe muscle tears with inability to move should be evaluated immediately by a doctor.
