Dementia | Alzheimer’s and Lewy Body Disease

Dementia is a progressive neurological disorder that affects memory, thinking, behavior, and the ability to perform daily activities. Among its various forms, **Alzheimer’s disease** is the most common, caused by abnormal protein deposits in the brain, while **Lewy Body Dementia (LBD)** involves both cognitive decline and movement disorders due to Lewy body protein clumps. Over time, dementia impacts both mental and physical independence, requiring long-term management and caregiving support.

Symptoms

Dementia symptoms develop gradually and vary between types:

  • Memory loss affecting daily life
  • Difficulty communicating or finding words
  • Disorientation in time or place
  • Decreased problem-solving and judgment skills
  • Frequent mood changes, confusion, or paranoia
  • Personality changes and social withdrawal
  • Visual hallucinations (common in Lewy Body dementia)
  • Parkinsonian symptoms such as tremors or stiffness
  • Sleep disturbances and fluctuating attention
  • Loss of ability to perform self-care activities

Causes

Dementia results from damage to brain cells that interferes with communication and memory formation. Major forms include:

  • Alzheimer’s Disease: Caused by beta-amyloid plaques and tau tangles disrupting brain communication.
  • Lewy Body Dementia: Caused by deposits of alpha-synuclein (Lewy bodies) affecting cognition and motor control.
  • Vascular Dementia: Related to reduced blood flow from strokes or small vessel disease.
  • Frontotemporal Dementia: Involves nerve cell loss in the frontal and temporal lobes, causing behavioral and speech changes.
  • Genetic predisposition and family history.
  • Chronic cardiovascular or metabolic diseases (hypertension, diabetes).
  • Brain injuries or prolonged alcohol use.

Children

  • Extremely rare; may occur from inherited metabolic or mitochondrial disorders.

Adults

  • Early-onset genetic Alzheimer’s due to mutations (APP, PSEN1, PSEN2).
  • Lifestyle risks: poor diet, hypertension, obesity, smoking, or head injury.

Aged People

  • Age over 65 is the strongest known risk factor.
  • Accumulation of amyloid and Lewy proteins increases with age.
  • Comorbid health issues accelerate decline.

Precautions | Preventions

  • Maintain regular physical activity and brain exercises.
  • Consume Mediterranean-style diet (rich in fish oil, fruits, nuts, olive oil).
  • Ensure proper sleep and manage stress.
  • Control cardiovascular conditions—keep blood pressure and sugar in check.
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol use.
  • Stay socially and intellectually engaged.

Treatment Options

Medical Treatment

  • Cholinesterase inhibitors (Donepezil, Rivastigmine, Galantamine) for mild to moderate dementia.
  • Memantine for moderate to severe Alzheimer’s to regulate glutamate function.
  • Antipsychotics for hallucinations (used cautiously in Lewy Body dementia).
  • Antidepressants and sleep aid therapy for behavioral symptoms.

Non-Medical Support

  • Cognitive stimulation therapy and memory exercises.
  • Caregiver education and home safety adjustments.
  • Speech therapy for communication issues.
  • Music, art, and reminiscence therapy to enhance emotional well-being.

Rehabilitation Focus

  • Preserve independence through daily structure and monitored routines.
  • Occupational therapy for self-care maintenance.

Ancient Treatments

In ancient healing practices, cognitive decline was addressed with herbal and lifestyle regimes. Ayurveda recommended **Brahmi, Ashwagandha, Shankhpushpi**, and meditation to enhance memory retention and concentration, while Traditional Chinese Medicine used **Ginkgo biloba and Panax ginseng** for improving cerebral circulation and clarity.

Natural Remedies for Cognitive Health

  • Turmeric (curcumin) may reduce amyloid plaque accumulation.
  • Omega-3 fatty acids to support brain cell repair.
  • Vitamin E and antioxidants to reduce oxidative stress.
  • Breathing exercises and yoga for brain oxygenation.
  • Regular meditation to enhance mindfulness and memory function.

⚠️ Natural interventions can support mental clarity but do not reverse dementia.
