Alzheimer’s Disease | Dementia Awareness

Alzheimer’s Disease is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that affects memory, thinking, and behavior. It is the most common cause of dementia in older adults and leads to gradual decline in cognitive ability and daily functioning. Although there is no cure, early diagnosis and treatment can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life.

Symptoms

Signs develop slowly and worsen over time as brain cell connections break down. Common symptoms include:

  • Memory loss disrupting daily life
  • Difficulty planning or solving problems
  • Confusion with time, place, or familiar faces
  • Struggling to find the right words or follow conversations
  • Misplacing things and inability to retrace steps
  • Loss of judgment and poor decision-making
  • Withdrawal from social and mental activities
  • Mood and personality changes, irritability, or aggression
  • Difficulty performing routine tasks
  • Visual or spatial challenges (trouble reading or judging distances)

Causes

Alzheimer’s Disease is caused by a combination of genetic, environmental, and lifestyle factors leading to brain cell death.

  • Abnormal buildup of amyloid plaques and tau tangles
  • Brain inflammation and oxidative stress
  • Genetic risk factors like APOE ε4 gene
  • Diabetes, high blood pressure, and obesity affecting brain blood flow
  • Lifestyle factors such as smoking, inactivity, or unhealthy diet
  • Head injuries or traumatic brain injury (TBI)
  • Age-related decline in neurotransmitters, especially acetylcholine

Children

  • Alzheimer’s is extremely rare in children; when present, it is usually due to genetic brain disorders.
  • Conditions like Niemann-Pick disease type C may mimic Alzheimer’s-like symptoms.

Adults

  • Onset often begins after age 60 but early-onset Alzheimer’s can appear in the 30s or 40s.
  • Genetic mutations or strong family history increase risk.
  • Chronic diseases, poor sleep, and depression accelerate decline.

Aged People

  • Primary risk group for Alzheimer’s due to natural brain degeneration.
  • Co-existing conditions like vascular disease or diabetes worsen progression.
  • Age-related oxidative stress increases amyloid plaque formation.

Precautions | Preventions

  • Maintain regular physical and mental exercise.
  • Adopt a Mediterranean or DASH diet with antioxidants and omega-3 foods.
  • Stay socially engaged and mentally challenged with games, reading, or hobbies.
  • Control blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar levels.
  • Ensure 7–8 hours of quality sleep every night.
  • Avoid smoking, alcohol, and heavy processed foods.
  • Manage stress through yoga, mindfulness, and relaxation techniques.
  • Regular health check-ups for cognitive screening.

Treatment Options

Medications

  • Cholinesterase inhibitors (Donepezil, Rivastigmine) to boost neurotransmitters.
  • Memantine (NMDA antagonist) to regulate glutamate activity.
  • New therapies targeting amyloid and tau protein buildup.

Non-Drug Therapies

  • Cognitive stimulation therapy and memory exercises.
  • Psychological counselling and caregiver support.
  • Routine physical activity to stabilize mood and cognition.

Emerging Treatments

  • Monoclonal antibody infusions like Lecanemab under clinical evaluation.
  • Stem cell and gene-editing therapies in research phase.
  • Neuroprotective supplements under investigation.
Treatment Benefits Limitations
Medication Improves cognition and temporarily slows decline. Does not stop or reverse the disease.
Therapy Enhances daily functioning and mood. Requires consistent effort and care support.
⚠️ Early diagnosis and therapy can significantly improve patient and caregiver quality of life.

Ancient Treatments

Ancient medicine often linked memory problems to imbalance of body fluids or “doshas.” Treatments included Ayurvedic herbs like Brahmi and Shankhpushpi, Chinese ginkgo leaves for memory enhancement, and meditation for brain clarity. While not curative, such methods supported mental calmness and concentration.

Natural Remedies for Alzheimer’s

Natural remedies can complement medical therapy to slow progression and improve brain health.

Dietary & Herbal Support

  • Include turmeric (curcumin), green tea, and berries rich in antioxidants.
  • Omega-3 fatty acids from fish oil help maintain neuronal integrity.
  • Ginkgo biloba and Ashwagandha support cognitive function.

Lifestyle Measures

  • Brain training puzzles and crosswords to enhance memory.
  • Regular walking, yoga, and breathing exercises.
  • Balanced diet with nuts, seeds, and leafy greens.

Natural remedies assist in care but do not replace professional therapy. Seek medical advice before using any herbal supplement.
