Pharyngitis | Sore Throat

Pharyngitis, commonly referred to as a sore throat, is the inflammation of the pharynx—the back of the throat. It is usually caused by viral or bacterial infections but may also result from allergies, pollutants, or irritants. Symptoms include throat pain, difficulty swallowing, and swollen glands. While most cases are mild and self-limiting, bacterial pharyngitis (including strep throat) requires medical care.

Symptoms

The symptoms of pharyngitis (sore throat) can include:

  • Pain or scratchy sensation in the throat
  • Difficulty or pain while swallowing
  • Red, swollen throat, sometimes with white patches
  • Swollen lymph nodes in the neck
  • Hoarseness or voice changes
  • Fever, headache, or fatigue (in infections)
  • Cough, runny nose (in viral pharyngitis)
  • Loss of appetite in children

Causes

Pharyngitis is most often caused by infections or irritants. Key causes include:

  • Viral infections (common cold, flu, mononucleosis, COVID-19)
  • Bacterial infections (Streptococcus group A – strep throat)
  • Allergies causing postnasal drip
  • Exposure to smoke, pollution, or chemical irritants
  • Dry air (especially in winters)
  • Voice strain from shouting or prolonged speaking
  • Acid reflux (GERD) irritating the throat

Children

  • Frequent viral colds at school
  • Bacterial strep throat spreading in groups
  • Low immunity and close contact environments

Adults

  • Stress and weakened immune system
  • Allergies, smoking, and environmental irritants
  • GERD leading to frequent sore throats

Aged People

  • Reduced immunity increasing infection risk
  • Chronic respiratory conditions aggravating throat inflammation
  • Dry mouth from medications

Precautions | Prevention

  • Wash hands frequently to prevent spread.
  • Avoid sharing cups, utensils, or personal items.
  • Stay hydrated and keep throat moist.
  • Limit exposure to smoke and pollutants.
  • Cover mouth when coughing or sneezing.
  • Manage acid reflux with diet and lifestyle changes.
  • Use humidifiers to combat dry air.

Treatment Options

Home Remedies

  • Gargling with warm salt water
  • Warm teas with honey, ginger, or lemon
  • Lozenges for throat soothing
  • Rest and hydration

Medications

  • Pain relievers like ibuprofen or acetaminophen
  • Antibiotics (only if bacterial pharyngitis/strep throat is diagnosed)
  • Antihistamines for allergy-related sore throats
  • Antacids or PPIs if sore throat is due to acid reflux

When to Seek Medical Care

  • Severe sore throat lasting more than a week
  • High fever or swollen lymph nodes
  • Difficulty breathing or swallowing
  • Frequent recurrent sore throats
⚠️ Strep throat requires immediate medical treatment with antibiotics to prevent complications like rheumatic fever.

Ancient Treatments

In ancient Ayurveda, sore throats were managed with turmeric milk, licorice root, holy basil (tulsi), and honey. Warm herbal gargles and steam inhalation were common practices, while Traditional Chinese Medicine used herbal teas and acupuncture to reduce throat inflammation.
