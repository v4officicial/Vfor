Gout | Uric Acid Arthritis

Gout is a form of arthritis caused by excess uric acid build-up in the blood, leading to needle-like crystals forming in the joints. It causes sudden, severe joint pain, swelling, and redness, often affecting the big toe. Gout attacks are extremely painful and recurrent if untreated, but manageable with lifestyle changes and medications.

Symptoms

Gout symptoms often come suddenly at night and include:

  • Severe joint pain, often in the big toe
  • Swelling, warmth, and redness around the joint
  • Lingering discomfort after acute attack
  • Limited range of motion in affected joint
  • Tophi (chalky uric acid deposits under skin in chronic gout)

Causes

Gout is primarily caused by hyperuricemia (excess uric acid in the blood), leading to crystal deposits in joints.

  • Overproduction or under-excretion of uric acid
  • Purine-rich diet (red meat, shellfish, organ meats)
  • Alcohol, especially beer
  • Obesity and metabolic syndrome
  • Kidney disease reducing uric acid clearance
  • Certain medications (diuretics, aspirin)
  • Genetics and family history

Children

  • Rare; may occur in inherited metabolic disorders
  • Pediatric gout linked to kidney disease or enzyme defects

Adults

  • Most common between 30–60 years
  • More frequent in men due to higher uric acid levels

Aged People

  • Common due to kidney decline and comorbidities
  • Polypharmacy (multiple drugs) increases gout risk

Precautions | Prevention

  • Maintain healthy weight and diet.
  • Limit purine-rich foods (red meat, shellfish, organ meats).
  • Reduce alcohol, especially beer and spirits.
  • Drink plenty of water to flush uric acid.
  • Avoid sugary drinks and high-fructose foods.
  • Manage blood pressure, diabetes, and cholesterol.

Treatment Options

Medications

  • NSAIDs: Relieve pain and inflammation during acute attacks.
  • Colchicine: Effective in reducing pain if taken early.
  • Corticosteroids: Used if NSAIDs/colchicine not suitable.
  • Urate-lowering therapy (Allopurinol, Febuxostat): Long-term prevention of recurrence.

Lifestyle & Diet

  • Hydration and low-purine diet
  • Weight management
  • Exercise and reduced alcohol intake

⚠️ Untreated gout may cause permanent joint damage and kidney stones.

Ancient Treatments

Ancient medicine described gout as the “disease of kings” due to its link with rich diets. Remedies included herbal decoctions, fasting, and leeches. Ayurveda recommended Guduchi and Triphala, while Traditional Chinese Medicine used acupuncture and anti-inflammatory herbs. Modern therapy, however, offers more effective and targeted treatment.

Natural Remedies for Gout

These support medical therapy and reduce flares:

  • Cherry juice (lowers uric acid and reduces inflammation)
  • Bananas and citrus fruits for vitamin C
  • Ginger and turmeric for pain relief
  • Apple cider vinegar soaks for swollen joints
  • Magnesium-rich foods to reduce uric acid formation
