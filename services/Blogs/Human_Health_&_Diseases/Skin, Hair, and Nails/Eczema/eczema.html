Eczema | Symptoms, Causes & Treatment

Eczema, also known as atopic dermatitis, is a common inflammatory skin condition characterized by itchy, dry, red, and irritated skin. It can occur at any age and often arises due to a combination of genetic and environmental factors causing skin barrier dysfunction and immune system activation.

Symptoms

Typical symptoms of eczema include:

  • Red, inflamed patches of skin
  • Intense itching
  • Dry, cracked, or scaly skin
  • Small, raised bumps that may leak fluid when scratched
  • Thickened or leathery skin in chronic cases
  • Risk of skin infections from scratching
  • Skin sensitivity and discomfort

Causes

Eczema results from a combination of genetic, environmental, and immunological factors, including:

  • Genetic predisposition causing impaired skin barrier
  • Environmental triggers such as allergens, irritants, pollution, or climate
  • Immune system overreaction causing inflammation
  • Dry skin and frequent exposure to water or harsh soaps
  • Stress, heat, or sweating as exacerbating factors
  • Allergic conditions such as asthma or hay fever
  • Contact with certain fabrics, detergents, or chemicals

Precautions | Preventions

  • Keep skin moisturized frequently with emollients
  • Avoid known allergens and irritants
  • Use gentle, fragrance-free skin care products
  • Wear soft, breathable cotton fabrics
  • Maintain moderate indoor humidity
  • Reduce scratching by keeping nails trimmed
  • Manage stress effectively through relaxation techniques
  • Avoid extreme temperatures and sweating

Treatment Options

Medications

  • Topical corticosteroids to reduce inflammation
  • Topical calcineurin inhibitors (Tacrolimus, Pimecrolimus)
  • Antihistamines to control itching
  • Antibiotics for secondary skin infections
  • Systemic immunosuppressants or biologics for severe cases

Skin Care

  • Use of moisturizers multiple times daily
  • Avoid hot showers and harsh soaps

Phototherapy

  • Ultraviolet (UV) light therapy recommended for chronic eczema

Comparison: Topical vs Systemic Treatments

TreatmentBenefitsLimitations
Topical Effective for mild to moderate cases, fewer systemic side effects Requires regular application, skin irritation possible
Systemic Helpful for severe or resistant eczema Potential systemic side effects, requires monitoring
⚠️ Follow medical advice strictly; uncontrolled eczema risks infection and scarring.

Ancient Treatments

Traditional medicine used herbal pastes, oils such as neem and turmeric, and dietary adjustments to soothe inflamed skin and promote healing. Ayurveda and Chinese medicine emphasize detoxification and balancing body energies to control eczema symptoms.

Natural Remedies for Eczema

Complementary natural therapies can support skin healing and symptom relief:

Home & Herbal Remedies

  • Coconut oil and aloe vera for moisturizing and soothing
  • Oatmeal baths to relieve itching
  • Calendula and chamomile creams
  • Avoid harsh detergents and scented products

Lifestyle Adjustments

  • Manage stress
  • Wear loose, breathable fabrics
  • Maintain proper hydration and balanced nutrition

Natural remedies assist but do not replace medical treatment. Consult your healthcare provider before trying new therapies.
