Acne | Symptoms, Causes & Treatment

Acne is a common skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells, resulting in pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads. It mainly affects teens but can occur at any age. Acne severity ranges from mild to cystic acne, which can cause scarring. Effective treatment depends on acne type and severity.

Symptoms

Acne symptoms include:

  • Whiteheads – small, flesh-colored bumps
  • Blackheads – open clogged pores appearing black due to oxidation
  • Pimples (papules and pustules) – inflamed red bumps or pus-filled lesions
  • Cysts and nodules – deep, painful, large lumps under the skin
  • Scarring and hyperpigmentation in severe cases
  • Oily skin
  • Redness and inflammation around lesions

Causes

Acne develops due to:

  • Excess sebum (oil) production by sebaceous glands
  • Clogged hair follicles with dead skin cells
  • Bacterial growth, especially Propionibacterium acnes
  • Hormonal fluctuations during puberty, menstruation, or pregnancy
  • Genetic predisposition
  • Certain medications (corticosteroids, lithium)
  • Stress and diet may aggravate acne
  • Use of comedogenic skin or hair products

Children and Teens

  • Acne commonly begins during puberty due to hormonal changes
  • Severity varies with genetics and skin type

Adults

  • Adult acne often linked to hormonal imbalance or stress
  • May persist from adolescence or develop later
  • More common in women due to menstrual cycle and hormonal contraceptives

Precautions | Preventions

  • Use non-comedogenic and gentle skin care products
  • Avoid excessive scrubbing or harsh treatments of skin
  • Maintain a balanced diet and hydration
  • Manage stress through relaxation and lifestyle
  • Avoid squeezing or picking at acne lesions
  • Regular cleansing but avoid overwashing

Treatment Options

Topical Treatments

  • Benzoyl peroxide and salicylic acid
  • Retinoids to unclog pores and reduce inflammation
  • Antibiotics to reduce bacteria and inflammation

Oral Medications

  • Antibiotics for moderate to severe acne
  • Hormonal therapies such as oral contraceptives
  • Isotretinoin for severe, cystic acne under supervision

Other Therapies

  • Light and laser therapy
  • Chemical peels and drainage procedures

Comparison: Topical vs Oral Treatments

TreatmentBenefitsLimitations
Topical Localized, fewer systemic side effects Requires prolonged use, skin irritation possible
Oral Effective for severe or widespread acne Systemic side effects, requires monitoring
⚠️ Consult dermatologist for personalized acne treatment especially in severe or persistent cases.

Ancient Treatments

Traditional remedies involve herbal oils, turmeric packs, and cleansing regimens. Ayurveda and other ancient systems used natural anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial herbs to improve skin health and reduce acne severity.

Natural Remedies for Acne

Complementary measures can improve mild acne and skin health:

Natural Treatments

  • Tea tree oil and aloe vera for antimicrobial and soothing effects
  • Honey and turmeric masks as anti-inflammatory applications
  • A balanced diet low in sugar and dairy
  • Proper skincare hygiene

Lifestyle & Dietary Recommendations

  • Reduce stress and ensure adequate sleep
  • Drink plenty of water
  • Avoid greasy or processed foods

Natural remedies complement but do not replace medical acne treatments. Consult your dermatologist before trying new products.
