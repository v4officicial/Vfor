Cryptococcosis | Fungal Infection

Cryptococcosis is a fungal infection caused by inhaling spores of *Cryptococcus neoformans* or *Cryptococcus gattii*. It primarily affects the lungs but can disseminate to the central nervous system causing meningitis. It commonly occurs in immunocompromised individuals, including those with HIV/AIDS.

Symptoms

Symptoms vary depending on the site of infection and immune status:

  • Lung infection: cough, chest pain, shortness of breath, fever
  • Meningitis: headache, fever, neck stiffness, confusion
  • Skin lesions: nodules, ulcers, papules
  • Fatigue and weight loss in disseminated infection

Causes

Cryptococcosis is caused by inhalation of airborne spores from environmental reservoirs such as pigeon droppings and decayed wood. Risk factors include:

  • Immunocompromised states (HIV/AIDS, cancer, transplant recipients)
  • Exposure to contaminated soil or bird droppings
  • Prolonged corticosteroid or immunosuppressive therapy
  • Chronic lung disease
  • Living in or traveling to endemic areas

Children

  • Rare but possible with immunodeficiency
  • Symptoms resemble adults

Adults

  • Most common in immunocompromised adults
  • Lung and CNS infections are critical
  • Disseminated disease more frequent in HIV/AIDS

Elderly

  • Higher risk due to decreased immunity
  • More severe and prolonged illness

Precautions | Prevention

  • Avoid exposure to pigeon droppings or bird habitats.
  • Use protective masks in dusty environments.
  • Maintain immune health and seek early HIV treatment.
  • Practice proper hygiene and avoid contaminated soil.

Treatment Options

Antifungal Therapy

  • Amphotericin B combined with flucytosine for initial severe meningitis.
  • Fluconazole for consolidation and maintenance phases.
  • Long treatment duration, often months to years.

Supportive Care

  • Management of increased intracranial pressure in meningitis.
  • Monitoring and supportive treatment of symptoms.

Comparison: Amphotericin B vs. Fluconazole

Medication Advantages Limitations
Amphotericin B - Potent antifungal
- Effective for severe infections including meningitis 		- Intravenous only
- Significant potential toxicity (kidney damage)
- Requires hospital care
Fluconazole - Oral medication
- Well-tolerated for maintenance therapy 		- Less effective alone for severe disease
- Requires prolonged therapy
⚠️ Early diagnosis and treatment essential to reduce morbidity and mortality.

Ancient Treatments

In ancient times, fungal infections of the lungs and skin were poorly understood and treated mainly with herbal remedies to reduce symptoms and boost immunity, such as decoctions of medicinal plants. Modern antifungal therapy dramatically improved outcomes.

Natural Remedies for Cryptococcosis

Natural remedies may help support immune system and symptom relief but do not cure cryptococcosis, which requires antifungal medication.

Supportive Natural Care

  • Herbal teas like ginger and turmeric to support immunity.
  • Maintaining good nutrition and hydration.
  • Steam inhalation to ease respiratory symptoms.

Always seek medical treatment for confirmed cryptococcal infection.
