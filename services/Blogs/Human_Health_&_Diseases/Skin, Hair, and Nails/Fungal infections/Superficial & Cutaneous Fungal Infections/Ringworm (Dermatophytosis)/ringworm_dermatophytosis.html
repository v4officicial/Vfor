Ringworm (Dermatophytosis)

Ringworm, medically called Dermatophytosis, is a contagious fungal infection affecting the skin, hair, and nails caused by dermatophyte fungi such as *Trichophyton*, *Microsporum*, and *Epidermophyton* species. It presents as circular, scaly, red rashes with raised edges, often accompanied by itching and discomfort.

Symptoms

Symptoms vary depending on the affected body area and include:

  • Round or oval red patches with raised, scaly borders (ring-shaped)
  • Itching and discomfort
  • Cracked, peeling, or scaling skin
  • Hair loss or broken hairs in scalp ringworm
  • Thickened, discolored nails in nail infections
  • Dry, scaly, and inflamed skin

Causes

Ringworm is caused by dermatophyte fungi infecting keratinized tissues. Common causes and risk factors include:

  • Fungi species: *Trichophyton rubrum*, *Microsporum canis*, *Epidermophyton floccosum*
  • Direct contact with infected persons, animals, or surfaces
  • Warm, humid environments promoting fungal growth
  • Poor hygiene and excessive sweating
  • Sharing contaminated clothing, towels, or sports equipment
  • Weakened immune system or chronic health conditions

Children

  • Scalp ringworm (tinea capitis) is common in children
  • Transmitted by close contact at schools or homes
  • May cause hair loss and scaly patches on the scalp

Adults

  • Tinea corporis (body), tinea cruris (groin), and tinea pedis (feet) are common
  • Higher risk with communal sports, gyms, and public showers

Elderly

  • Increased risk due to weakened skin barrier and circulation
  • More prone to nail fungus and chronic skin infections

Precautions | Prevention

  • Maintain good personal hygiene.
  • Avoid sharing personal items like towels and clothing.
  • Keep skin dry and clean, especially in moist areas.
  • Wear footwear in communal showers or locker rooms.
  • Clean and disinfect sports equipment regularly.
  • Promptly treat athlete’s foot and other fungal infections.

Treatment Options

Topical Antifungal Treatment

  • Clotrimazole, miconazole, terbinafine creams applied 2-4 weeks.
  • Selenium sulfide or ketoconazole shampoos for scalp infections.

Oral Antifungal Treatments

  • Griseofulvin or terbinafine tablets for 4-8 weeks, especially for scalp and nail infections.
  • Itraconazole as an alternative oral therapy.

Ancient Treatments

Traditional treatments relied on herbal pastes using neem, turmeric, and aloe vera for antifungal and soothing effects. Sunlight exposure and hygiene management were important to keep fungal growth in check, though modern antifungal medications ensure faster cure rates.

Natural Remedies for Ringworm

Natural remedies can help relieve symptoms and act as complementary care alongside medications.

Supportive Natural Care

  • Tea tree oil for its antifungal properties applied topically.
  • Aloe vera gel for soothing inflamed skin.
  • Turmeric paste with antibacterial and antifungal action.
  • Garlic paste may help reduce fungal load.

Natural remedies should not replace antifungal medications prescribed by a healthcare provider.
