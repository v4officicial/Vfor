Candidiasis | Yeast Infection

Candidiasis is a fungal infection caused primarily by Candida albicans and other Candida species, affecting skin, mucous membranes, and internal organs in severe cases. It commonly manifests as oral thrush, vaginal yeast infection, or skin infection, especially in people with weakened immunity, diabetes, or antibiotic use.

Symptoms

Candidiasis presents with variable symptoms depending on site of infection:

  • Oral thrush: White patches in mouth, soreness, difficulty swallowing.
  • Vaginal yeast infection: Itching, redness, whitish discharge, burning sensation.
  • Skin candidiasis: Red, itchy rash usually in moist folds (armpits, groin).
  • Diaper rash: Red, inflamed skin in diaper area of infants.
  • Invasive candidiasis: Fever, chills, systemic symptoms in hospitalized or immunocompromised patients.

Causes

Candidiasis is caused by overgrowth of Candida species, normally present on skin and mucous membranes. Factors causing imbalance include:

  • Antibiotic or corticosteroid use
  • Weakened immune system (HIV, cancer, diabetes)
  • High sugar diet or uncontrolled diabetes
  • Warm and moist environments aiding fungal growth
  • Hormonal changes during pregnancy or oral contraceptive use
  • Poor oral hygiene or denture use
  • Prolonged hospitalization or catheter use

Children

  • Commonly causes oral thrush and diaper rash
  • Immature immune systems predispose to infection
  • Can cause feeding difficulties

Adults

  • Risk increased with diabetes, pregnancy, HIV, or antibiotic use
  • Vaginal candidiasis common in women of reproductive age
  • Skin infection in obese or bedridden patients

Elderly

  • Greater risk due to immunosenescence and chronic illnesses
  • Oral and skin candidiasis more frequent
  • May complicate other health conditions

Precautions | Prevention

  • Maintain good oral and personal hygiene.
  • Limit unnecessary antibiotic and corticosteroid use.
  • Control blood sugar levels if diabetic.
  • Keep skin folds dry and clean.
  • Wear breathable, loose-fitting clothing.
  • Use antifungal powders in humid environments.
  • Proper denture cleaning daily.
  • Seek early treatment for persistent fungal symptoms.

Treatment Options

Topical Treatments

  • Nystatin suspension or ointment for oral and skin candidiasis.
  • Clotrimazole or miconazole creams for skin and vaginal candidiasis.

Oral/Systemic Treatments

  • Fluconazole oral tablets for moderate to severe cases.
  • Itraconazole or amphotericin B for invasive infections.
  • Longer duration treatment in immunocompromised patients.

Supportive Care

  • Pain relief for oral or skin lesions.
  • Good hydration and nutrition.

Ancient Treatments

Traditional treatments for candidiasis included herbal rinses, neem and turmeric pastes, and cow’s milk applications for oral thrush. Ayurveda and folk medicine emphasized balancing body doshas and topical herbal therapies to reduce fungal overgrowth.

Natural Remedies for Candidiasis

Natural remedies support conventional treatments by soothing irritated skin and restoring microbiome balance.

Natural Supportive Measures

  • Probiotic foods like yogurt to restore healthy flora.
  • Coconut oil for topical antifungal effects.
  • Garlic with antifungal properties included in diet.
  • Aloe vera gel for soothing irritated skin.
  • Tea tree oil diluted for external candidiasis.

Natural options complement but do not replace antifungal medications.
