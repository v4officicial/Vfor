Alopecia (Hair Loss) | Symptoms, Causes & Treatment

Alopecia refers to hair loss from the scalp or other parts of the body. It can be temporary or permanent and results from various causes including genetics, autoimmune conditions, hormonal changes, infections, or nutritional deficiencies. Early diagnosis and appropriate treatment can improve outcomes and promote hair regrowth.

Symptoms

Signs of alopecia include:

  • Gradual thinning on the top of scalp (male/female pattern baldness)
  • Patchy hair loss in round or oval shapes (alopecia areata)
  • Sudden loosening of hair causing shedding
  • Overall thinning or bald spots
  • Itching, scaling, or redness of the scalp in some cases
  • Loss of hair in other parts of the body (eyebrows, eyelashes)

Causes

Common causes of alopecia include:

  • Androgenetic alopecia (genetic hair loss)
  • Alopecia areata (autoimmune disorder)
  • Telogen effluvium due to stress, illness, or medications
  • Hormonal changes (pregnancy, menopause, thyroid disorders)
  • Scalp infections (fungal or bacterial)
  • Physical or emotional stress
  • Nutritional deficiencies (iron, zinc, protein)
  • Certain medications and chemotherapy
  • Traction alopecia from hairstyles pulling hair

Children

  • Alopecia areata and tinea capitis (fungal infection) most common causes
  • Nutritional issues and autoimmune disorders

Adults

  • Hereditary pattern baldness is predominant
  • Hormonal and autoimmune causes also contribute
  • Stress and illnesses can trigger hair loss episodes

Aged People

  • Hair thinning due to aging (senescent alopecia)
  • Chronic medical conditions and medications

Precautions | Preventions

  • Avoid hairstyles that pull hair tightly
  • Maintain a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Manage stress through relaxation and exercise
  • Avoid harsh hair treatments and products
  • Regular scalp care and hygiene
  • Early consultation for unexplained hair loss

Treatment Options

Medications

  • Minoxidil topical applications to stimulate growth
  • Oral finasteride for male pattern baldness
  • Corticosteroids injections for alopecia areata

Procedures

  • Hair transplant surgery
  • Laser therapy for hair growth stimulation
  • Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) injections

Home & Lifestyle

  • Healthy diet and vitamin supplementation
  • Stress management techniques
  • Avoidance of damaging hair practices

Comparison: Medical vs Surgical Treatments

TreatmentBenefitsLimitations
Medical Non-invasive, easy to use, stimulates hair growth May take months to show effect, not permanent
Surgical Permanent restoration, natural appearance Costly, surgical risks, limited donor hair
⚠️ Early intervention improves outcomes; consult a dermatologist for personalized care.

Ancient Treatments

Ancient remedies included the use of natural oils (coconut, castor), herbal extracts (amla, bhringraj), and scalp massages to promote hair health and reduce hair loss. These traditional practices supported circulation and hair follicle nourishment.

Natural Remedies for Alopecia

Complementary natural approaches include:

Diet & Herbal Support

  • Balanced diet with protein, iron, vitamins A, C, D, and E
  • Herbal treatments including rosemary oil, aloe vera, and green tea extracts
  • Omega-3 fatty acids for hair follicle nourishment

Lifestyle & Care

  • Gentle hair care routine
  • Avoid heat styling and chemical treatments
  • Stress reduction through yoga and meditation

Natural remedies can support but not substitute medical treatments; consult healthcare before starting new therapies.
