Psoriasis | Chronic Skin Inflammation

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune skin disorder that causes rapid skin cell buildup leading to thick, scaly patches of skin. It usually appears on the scalp, elbows, knees, and lower back. Psoriasis is not contagious but can cause significant discomfort and emotional distress. The condition tends to go through cycles, flaring up for weeks or months, then subsiding.

Symptoms

Psoriasis symptoms vary depending on the type and severity, but common signs include:

  • Thick, raised, red patches covered with silvery-white scales
  • Dry, cracked skin that may bleed or itch
  • Burning or soreness around affected areas
  • Thickened, ridged, or pitted nails (nail psoriasis)
  • Joint stiffness and swelling (psoriatic arthritis)
  • Flaky scalp layers resembling dandruff
  • Inflamed patches that flare up during stress, infections, or cold weather

Causes

Psoriasis results from an overactive immune system that speeds up the life cycle of skin cells. Instead of shedding, cells accumulate on the surface, forming itchy, scaly plaques.

  • Immune system malfunction causing overproduction of skin cells
  • Genetic predisposition or family history of psoriasis
  • Environmental triggers like cold, dry weather or skin injury
  • Emotional stress and hormonal changes
  • Infections such as strep throat or skin infections
  • Certain medications (beta-blockers, lithium, antimalarials)
  • Excessive alcohol, smoking, or obesity increasing risk

Children

  • Triggered often after throat infections like strep throat
  • Guttate psoriasis with small red drop-like lesions common in young age
  • Family history significantly increases risk

Adults

  • Chronic plaques often appear on joints, scalp, and trunk
  • Associated with metabolic disorders and chronic stress
  • Psoriatic arthritis risk increases with age

Aged People

  • Weakened immunity may worsen psoriasis severity
  • Joint inflammation and stiffness more common
  • Risk of skin dryness and sensitivity to treatments

Precautions | Preventions

  • Maintain moisturized skin using thick creams, oils, or ointments daily.
  • Avoid harsh soaps and fragrances that irritate the skin.
  • Eat anti-inflammatory foods rich in omega-3s, fruits, and green vegetables.
  • Manage stress through yoga, meditation, or breathing exercises.
  • Stay hydrated and maintain a healthy weight.
  • Limit alcohol and avoid smoking.
  • Protect skin from injury and infections.
  • Get adequate sunlight exposure or phototherapy under guidance.

Treatment Options

Topical Treatments

  • Corticosteroid creams to reduce inflammation and itching
  • Vitamin D analogs (calcipotriol) to slow skin cell growth
  • Coal tar and salicylic acid for scaling control
  • Moisturizers and keratolytic agents for hydration

Phototherapy

  • Controlled exposure to UVB light helps reduce patches
  • PUVA therapy (Psoralen + UVA) for severe cases

Systemic Medications

  • Methotrexate or cyclosporine for immune suppression
  • Biologic injections (adalimumab, etanercept, ustekinumab) for severe psoriasis

Lifestyle and Counseling

  • Regular counseling for mental health and self-esteem
  • Adherence to dermatologist-prescribed routines

Ancient Treatments

In traditional Ayurvedic and herbal medicine, psoriasis was treated with detoxification and natural oils. Therapies included Panchakarma, herbal baths, and neem, turmeric, or aloe vera-based applications to soothe the skin.

Natural Remedies for Psoriasis

  • Aloe vera gel for soothing inflammation and dryness
  • Coconut oil or olive oil applied to reduce scaling
  • Turmeric supplements or paste for antioxidant effects
  • Oatmeal baths to relieve itching and redness
  • Dead Sea salt baths to exfoliate and calm skin
  • Diet rich in antioxidants, omega-3s, and herbal teas
