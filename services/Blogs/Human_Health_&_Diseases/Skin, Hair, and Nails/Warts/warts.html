Warts | Symptoms, Causes & Treatment

Warts are small, rough skin growths caused by infection with the human papillomavirus (HPV). They commonly appear on hands, feet, and other body parts but can occur anywhere on the skin or mucous membranes. While often harmless, warts can be contagious and sometimes cause discomfort or cosmetic concern.

Symptoms

Signs of warts vary depending on type, but typically include:

  • Small, grainy bumps with rough texture
  • Black or brown dots inside growth (clotted blood vessels)
  • Raised or flat growths
  • Occasional pain or tenderness (especially plantar warts)
  • Clusters or isolated warts
  • Location specific symptoms, e.g. genital warts may cause itching or discomfort

Causes

Warts are caused by infection with specific types of human papillomavirus (HPV):

  • Entry of HPV virus through breaks in the skin
  • Direct contact with warts or contaminated surfaces
  • Sharing personal items like towels or razors can spread infection
  • Weakened immune system increases susceptibility
  • Exposure to moist environments such as public pools or showers
  • Children, teenagers, and people with immune disorders are more prone

Precautions | Preventions

  • Avoid direct contact with warts on others or your own body
  • Do not share personal items like towels, shoes, or razors
  • Keep feet dry and wear protective footwear in public areas
  • Practice good hygiene
  • Avoid biting nails or picking warts
  • Promptly treat plantar or problematic warts

Treatment Options

At-Home Treatments

  • Salicylic acid preparations (creams, gels, patches)
  • Duct tape occlusion therapy
  • Over-the-counter freezing sprays

Medical Treatments

  • Cryotherapy with liquid nitrogen by healthcare professionals
  • Cantharidin application causing blister to lift wart
  • Surgical removal via excision or curettage
  • Electrosurgery or laser treatments
  • Immunotherapy injections (e.g., Candida antigen)

Comparison: At-Home vs Medical Treatments

TreatmentBenefitsLimitations
At-Home Convenient, cost-effective, non-invasive May take weeks to months, not always effective
Medical More effective, faster wart removal Potential pain, scarring, requires clinic visits
⚠️ See a doctor for warts that are painful, change appearance, spread rapidly, or do not improve with treatment.

Ancient Treatments

Traditional medicine utilized herbal pastes, plant extracts, and poultices to treat warts. Plants like aloe vera, garlic, and fig sap have been used historically for their antiviral and skin-healing properties.

Natural Remedies for Warts

Natural remedies may soothe skin and promote wart resolution:

Home Remedies

  • Apple cider vinegar topical application
  • Tea tree oil with antiviral properties
  • Garlic paste as an antiviral agent
  • Vitamin C application or supplements to boost immunity

Lifestyle Practices

  • Maintain clean, dry skin
  • Boost immune health via balanced nutrition and sleep

Natural remedies should complement, not replace, medical treatment for persistent or problematic warts. Consult healthcare providers before use.
