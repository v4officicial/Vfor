Intestinal Obstruction | Bowel Blockage

Intestinal obstruction is a blockage that prevents the normal passage of food, fluids, and gas through the intestines. It may occur in the small intestine or large intestine and can be partial or complete. This is a medical emergency that may lead to severe complications if not treated promptly.

Symptoms

  • Severe abdominal pain and cramps
  • Abdominal swelling (bloating)
  • Vomiting (sometimes with fecal matter)
  • Constipation or inability to pass stool or gas
  • Loss of appetite
  • Diarrhea in partial obstruction cases
  • Fever and dehydration in advanced cases

Causes

  • Adhesions (scar tissue after abdominal surgery)
  • Hernias trapping part of the intestine
  • Colon cancer or tumors in the intestine
  • Infections or Crohn’s disease causing inflammation
  • Twisting of the intestine (volvulus)
  • Intussusception (one part of intestine slides into another)
  • Severe constipation or impacted stool

Precautions | Preventions

  • Avoid excessive straining during bowel movements.
  • Eat a high-fiber diet to prevent constipation.
  • Drink plenty of fluids for smooth digestion.
  • Seek treatment for intestinal issues like Crohn’s disease early.
  • Maintain healthy body weight to lower hernia risk.
  • Regular medical checkups if you have a history of abdominal surgery.

Treatment Options

Non-Surgical

  • Hospitalization with IV fluids and electrolyte balance
  • Nasogastric tube insertion to relieve pressure and vomiting
  • Medications to ease symptoms and promote bowel movement

Surgical

  • Removal of obstruction such as tumor, adhesion, or hernia repair
  • Resection of damaged intestine if blood flow is cut off

⚠️ Intestinal obstruction is life-threatening and requires urgent medical care.

Ancient Approaches

In ancient medicine, bowel blockages were addressed with herbal purgatives, enemas, fasting, and oil-based massages. However, severe obstructions often led to fatal outcomes without surgery.

Natural Remedies (Supportive)

  • Fiber-rich diet for prevention of constipation-related obstructions
  • Ginger tea and peppermint for relieving bloating
  • Castor oil used in traditional remedies (not advised for complete blockages)
  • Probiotics to maintain gut health

⚠️ Natural remedies may help only in mild constipation; true intestinal obstruction always requires medical attention.
