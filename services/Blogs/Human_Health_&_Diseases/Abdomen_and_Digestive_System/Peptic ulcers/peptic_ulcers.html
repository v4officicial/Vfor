Peptic Ulcers | Stomach & Duodenal Sores

Peptic ulcers are open sores that develop on the inner lining of the stomach (gastric ulcers) or upper part of the small intestine (duodenal ulcers). They occur when the protective lining is weakened by stomach acid and digestive juices, often due to bacterial infection or long-term use of painkillers.

Symptoms

Common signs of peptic ulcers include:

  • Burning stomach pain (often worse when fasting or at night)
  • Indigestion, heartburn, or acid reflux
  • Feeling bloated or full after small meals
  • Loss of appetite and unintended weight loss
  • Nausea or vomiting (sometimes with blood)
  • Dark, tarry stools indicating bleeding
  • Fatigue due to slow blood loss leading to anemia

Causes

Main causes of peptic ulcers include:

  • Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) bacterial infection
  • Long-term use of NSAIDs like ibuprofen or aspirin
  • Excessive acid production due to stress, smoking, or alcohol
  • Rare conditions like Zollinger-Ellison syndrome (excess acid-producing tumor)

Precautions | Preventions

  • Avoid overuse of painkillers (NSAIDs) without prescription.
  • Limit alcohol and quit smoking to reduce stomach irritation.
  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals to lower acid build-up.
  • Manage stress with yoga, meditation, or relaxation techniques.
  • Maintain a healthy diet with fruits, vegetables, and probiotics.
  • Seek early medical help for persistent abdominal pain.

Treatment Options

Medications

  • Antibiotics to eliminate H. pylori infection
  • Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) to reduce stomach acid
  • H2-receptor blockers to control acid production
  • Protective medications like sucralfate to coat the ulcer

Lifestyle Management

  • Avoid smoking, alcohol, and excessive caffeine
  • Adopt a fiber-rich diet and manage stress effectively

Complications Management

  • Endoscopic therapy for bleeding ulcers
  • Surgery in rare, severe or non-healing cases

Ancient Treatments

In ancient times, peptic ulcers were treated with herbal infusions such as licorice root, honey, aloe vera, and milk-based diets. While these offered some relief, they rarely cured the root cause like bacterial infection.

Natural Remedies for Peptic Ulcers

While medical treatment is essential, natural remedies can aid recovery:

  • Consume probiotics like yogurt, kefir, or fermented foods
  • Honey, turmeric, and licorice root for anti-inflammatory and protective effects
  • Avoid spicy, fried, and very acidic foods that worsen pain
  • Stay hydrated and eat fiber-rich meals

⚠️ Natural remedies help relieve discomfort but should not replace medical care.
