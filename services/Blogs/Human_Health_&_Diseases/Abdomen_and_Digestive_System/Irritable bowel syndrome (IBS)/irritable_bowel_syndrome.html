Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) | Digestive Disorder

Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) is a common functional disorder of the digestive system that causes abdominal pain, bloating, gas, diarrhea, and constipation. Unlike structural diseases, IBS does not permanently damage the intestines but significantly impacts quality of life. It is a chronic condition requiring long-term management.

Symptoms

  • Abdominal cramps or pain (relieved after bowel movement)
  • Bloating and excess gas
  • Diarrhea, constipation, or alternating between both
  • Mucus in stools
  • Urgent need to pass stools
  • Feeling of incomplete evacuation
  • Symptoms worsen after meals or stress

Causes

Possible triggers and causes of IBS include:

  • Abnormal gut motility and sensitivity
  • Stress, anxiety, and mental health conditions
  • Changes in gut microbiota (dysbiosis)
  • History of gastrointestinal infections
  • Food intolerances (spicy foods, caffeine, dairy, gluten)
  • Genetic predisposition to gut sensitivity

Precautions | Lifestyle Management

  • Follow a low-FODMAP diet (reducing fermentable carbohydrates).
  • Eat smaller, frequent meals to ease digestion.
  • Avoid trigger foods: caffeine, alcohol, fatty or spicy foods.
  • Stay hydrated and exercise regularly.
  • Manage stress through meditation or yoga.
  • Keep a food diary to identify and avoid personal food triggers.

Treatment Options

Medications

  • Antispasmodics to relieve cramps
  • Laxatives for constipation-predominant IBS
  • Antidiarrheal drugs for diarrhea-predominant IBS
  • Probiotics and antibiotics (rifaximin) for gut microbiota balance
  • Low-dose antidepressants to manage pain and stress-associated IBS

Dietary and Lifestyle Therapies

  • Low-FODMAP diet
  • High-fiber diet for constipation-predominant cases
  • Regular exercise to support digestive health

Ancient Approaches

In traditional medicine, IBS-like symptoms were managed using herbal teas (peppermint, fennel), yoga, ayurvedic gut-balancing formulations, and stress-relief practices. While partially effective, they lacked structured diagnosis.

Natural Remedies for IBS

  • Probiotic supplements to balance intestinal bacteria
  • Peppermint oil capsules for pain relief
  • Fennel tea and ginger tea for bloating and cramps
  • Mind-body interventions: yoga, meditation, CBT therapy

⚠️ Natural remedies may reduce discomfort but should not replace medical care in severe IBS.
