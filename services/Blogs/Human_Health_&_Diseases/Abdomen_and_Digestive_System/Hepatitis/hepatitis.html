Hepatitis | Liver Inflammation

Hepatitis refers to inflammation of the liver, most commonly caused by viral infections (Hepatitis A, B, C, D, and E). It can also result from alcohol abuse, toxins, medications, or autoimmune conditions. Chronic hepatitis may lead to cirrhosis, liver failure, or liver cancer if untreated.

Symptoms

  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes)
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Loss of appetite
  • Dark urine and pale stools
  • Abdominal pain, especially in the liver area
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Mild fever
  • Joint pain (in hepatitis B and C)
  • Swelling in legs or abdomen in advanced stages

Causes

  • Viral Hepatitis (A, B, C, D, E viruses)
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Certain medications and toxins
  • Autoimmune hepatitis (immune system attacks liver)
  • Unhygienic food and water (esp. Hepatitis A and E)
  • Unsafe sex or blood transfusion (Hepatitis B and C)

Precautions | Prevention

  • Get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and B.
  • Avoid sharing needles, razors, or blood-related tools.
  • Practice safe sex and use barrier protection.
  • Avoid contaminated food and water.
  • Limit alcohol intake to protect the liver.
  • Wash hands regularly and maintain hygiene.
  • Regular blood tests if at high risk.

Treatment Options

Medical Treatment

  • Antiviral medications for Hepatitis B and C
  • Vaccines available for Hepatitis A and B (preventive)
  • Supportive care for acute cases (hydration, rest)
  • Liver transplant in end-stage cases

Lifestyle & Supportive Care

  • Stop alcohol completely
  • Adopt a liver-friendly balanced diet
  • Regular monitoring of liver enzymes

Ancient Approaches

Traditionally, hepatitis-like conditions (jaundice) were managed with herbal decoctions like turmeric, amla, kutki, milk thistle, and bitter gourd juices. While supportive, they could not cure viral hepatitis infections.

Natural Remedies (Supportive)

  • Turmeric milk and herbal liver tonics
  • Milk thistle and amla extracts to detoxify the liver
  • Avoid fatty, oily, and processed foods
  • Stay hydrated with water and fresh fruit juices

⚠️ Natural remedies help manage but cannot replace medical treatment for viral hepatitis.
