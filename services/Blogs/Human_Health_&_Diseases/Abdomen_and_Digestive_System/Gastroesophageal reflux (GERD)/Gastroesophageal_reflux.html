Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) | Acid Reflux Disorder

GERD happens when stomach acid frequently flows back into the esophagus (food pipe). This backwash, known as acid reflux, irritates the lining of the esophagus and causes symptoms like heartburn and regurgitation. Chronic GERD can lead to complications such as esophagitis, Barrett’s esophagus, or even esophageal cancer in rare cases.

Symptoms

  • Burning sensation in chest (heartburn), especially after meals
  • Regurgitation of sour liquid or food
  • Difficult or painful swallowing (dysphagia)
  • Chronic cough, sore throat, or hoarseness
  • Worsening symptoms when lying down at night
  • Feeling of a lump in the throat
  • Bad breath or dry cough

Causes

  • Weak or relaxed lower esophageal sphincter (LES)
  • Obesity and high abdominal pressure
  • Hiatal hernia (stomach pushes into chest cavity)
  • Heavy consumption of spicy, fried, or acidic foods
  • Excess caffeine, alcohol, or smoking
  • Pregnancy due to hormonal changes and abdominal pressure
  • Chronic use of certain medications (NSAIDs, calcium channel blockers)

Precautions | Lifestyle Tips

  • Maintain a healthy weight and avoid overeating.
  • Eat small, frequent meals instead of large, heavy meals.
  • Avoid lying down immediately after meals (wait 2–3 hours).
  • Avoid spicy, fried, and chocolate-rich foods.
  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol or caffeine intake.
  • Elevate the head of the bed to reduce nighttime reflux.

Treatment Options

Medications

  • Antacids for quick relief
  • H2 receptor blockers (famotidine)
  • Proton pump inhibitors (omeprazole, pantoprazole)

Advanced Treatment

  • Endoscopic therapy for severe GERD
  • Surgery (fundoplication) in chronic, resistant GERD

Ancient Approaches

In Ayurveda and traditional medicine, reflux was managed with cooling herbs like aloe vera, licorice, amla, and dietary moderation. Yoga postures like Vajrasana after meals were recommended for digestion support.

Natural Remedies (Supportive)

  • Consume ginger tea and chamomile tea to calm reflux
  • Chew fennel seeds after meals
  • Avoid late-night eating and stay upright after meals
  • Practice yoga and deep breathing for digestive health

⚠️ Natural methods ease discomfort but persistent GERD requires medical consultation.
