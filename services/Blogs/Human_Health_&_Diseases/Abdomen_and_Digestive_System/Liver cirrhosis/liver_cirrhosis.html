Liver Cirrhosis | Chronic Liver Damage

Liver cirrhosis is a progressive disease in which healthy liver tissue is replaced with scar tissue, leading to loss of function. It is often the result of chronic alcohol use, viral hepatitis, or non-alcoholic fatty liver disease. Advanced cirrhosis can cause life-threatening complications like liver failure and portal hypertension.

Symptoms

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Loss of appetite and weight loss
  • Yellowing of skin and eyes (jaundice)
  • Swelling in abdomen (ascites) and legs (edema)
  • Easy bruising and bleeding
  • Confusion, memory problems (hepatic encephalopathy)
  • Spider-like blood vessels on skin
  • Dark urine and pale stool

Causes

  • Chronic alcohol abuse
  • Chronic hepatitis B or hepatitis C infection
  • Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD)
  • Autoimmune hepatitis
  • Inherited diseases like Wilson’s disease, hemochromatosis
  • Long-term exposure to toxins or certain medications
  • Bile duct diseases leading to blocked bile flow

Precautions | Preventions

  • Avoid alcohol completely if diagnosed with liver disease.
  • Maintain a healthy weight and manage diabetes.
  • Get vaccinated against hepatitis A and B.
  • Eat a low-salt, balanced diet to prevent swelling.
  • Regular liver function check-ups if you are at risk.
  • Avoid unnecessary medications that burden the liver.

Treatment Options

Medical Treatment

  • Medications to reduce hepatitis infections or autoimmune diseases
  • Drugs to control high blood pressure in veins (portal hypertension)
  • Diuretics to reduce excess fluid buildup (ascites)

Lifestyle & Supportive Care

  • Complete avoidance of alcohol and harmful drugs
  • Low-sodium diet and proper nutrition
  • Liver function monitoring and regular checkups

Advanced Treatment

  • Endoscopic treatment for variceal bleeding
  • Liver transplant is the definitive treatment for advanced cirrhosis

Ancient Treatments

Historically, jaundice and liver ailments were treated with herbal tonics, detoxifying diets, yoga, and Ayurvedic formulations like turmeric, amla, and kutki. While supportive, they could not reverse advanced cirrhosis.

Natural Remedies for Liver Cirrhosis

  • Milk thistle (silymarin) for liver support
  • Turmeric and amla for antioxidant effect
  • Green tea for liver detox support
  • Avoid fatty, fried, and processed foods
  • Eat fresh vegetables, fruits, and whole grains

⚠️ Natural remedies support but cannot cure liver cirrhosis. Medical supervision is essential.
