Gastritis | Stomach Inflammation

Gastritis is the inflammation of the stomach lining caused by irritation, infection, or prolonged use of certain medications. It can appear suddenly (acute gastritis) or gradually over time (chronic gastritis). If left untreated, chronic gastritis may increase the risk of ulcers and stomach cancer.

Symptoms

  • Upper abdominal pain or discomfort (burning or gnawing)
  • Bloating and frequent belching
  • Nausea and vomiting (sometimes with blood)
  • Loss of appetite
  • Dark or tarry stools (sign of bleeding)
  • Indigestion, heartburn, or acid reflux
  • Hiccups and early feeling of fullness after meals

Causes

  • Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) bacterial infection
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Frequent use of painkillers (NSAIDs)
  • Spicy, acidic, or fried foods intake
  • Autoimmune gastritis
  • Severe stress or traumatic injury
  • Bile reflux into the stomach

Precautions | Lifestyle Tips

  • Eat smaller, frequent meals to reduce stomach irritation.
  • Avoid alcohol, spicy, fried, and acidic foods.
  • Limit or avoid painkillers like ibuprofen and aspirin.
  • Quit smoking and reduce caffeine intake.
  • Maintain good hygiene to prevent H. pylori infection.
  • Manage stress with yoga, meditation, and exercise.

Treatment Options

Medications

  • Antibiotics to treat H. pylori
  • Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs) for acid reduction
  • H2 receptor blockers to lower stomach acid
  • Antacids for quick relief

Supportive Measures

  • Adopt a mild and balanced diet
  • Avoid alcohol and smoking
  • Regular medical check-ups for chronic or recurring gastritis

Ancient Approaches

In Ayurveda and traditional medicine, gastritis was managed using herbs like licorice root, amla, aloe vera juice, and cooling diets that reduced stomach irritation. Fasting and calming teas were prescribed for acute episodes.

Natural Remedies (Supportive)

  • Drink chamomile or peppermint tea to reduce bloating
  • Aloe vera juice to soothe irritation
  • Probiotics like yogurt to restore gut balance
  • Honey and turmeric for anti-inflammatory effects
  • Ginger tea for nausea relief

⚠️ These may ease gastritis but medical care is essential if symptoms are chronic or severe.
