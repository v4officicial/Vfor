Periodontitis | Advanced Gum Disease

Periodontitis is a severe gum infection that damages the soft tissue and destroys the bone supporting teeth. It is a progression of untreated gingivitis, leading to loose teeth and even tooth loss. Early detection and professional treatment can prevent permanent damage caused by this chronic dental condition.

Symptoms

Periodontitis symptoms worsen over time if untreated. Common signs include:

  • Swollen, tender, and bleeding gums
  • Persistent bad breath or bad taste
  • Gums pulling away (receding) from the teeth
  • Loose or shifting teeth
  • Pain while chewing
  • Pus between gums and teeth
  • Changes in bite alignment

Causes

Periodontitis develops when plaque and calculus (tartar) accumulate, causing bacterial infection that destroys gums and bone.

  • Untreated gingivitis due to poor oral hygiene
  • Smoking and tobacco use
  • Diabetes and uncontrolled blood sugar levels
  • Genetic predisposition to gum disease
  • Weakened immunity from health conditions
  • Vitamin C deficiency
  • Certain medications affecting saliva flow

Children

  • Rare in children but may occur with poor dental care
  • Genetic or immune-related gum issues
  • Early onset in adolescents with aggressive form

Adults

  • Chronic plaque buildup
  • Smoking or chewing tobacco
  • Stress weakening immune response
  • Poorly managed diabetes

Aged People

  • Long-term plaque exposure leading to bone loss
  • Dry mouth due to multiple medications
  • Reduced healing capacity of gums
  • Higher risk of severe tooth loss

Precautions | Preventions

  • Maintain daily brushing and flossing habits.
  • Avoid tobacco in all forms.
  • Schedule professional dental cleanings twice a year.
  • Manage chronic illnesses like diabetes effectively.
  • Limit sugary foods and drinks.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Use mouthwash to reduce bacterial load.

Treatment Options

Professional Dental Procedures

  • Scaling and root planing (deep cleaning)
  • Flap surgery (gum lift) to clean roots
  • Bone grafts to restore lost jawbone
  • Tissue grafts to repair receding gums
  • Guided tissue regeneration using mesh membranes

Medication Support

  • Antibacterial mouth rinses
  • Antibiotic gels applied into gum pockets
  • Oral antibiotics for severe infection

For Severe Cases

  • Laser-assisted gum therapy
  • Regenerative procedures to regrow bone
  • Extraction and replacement with implants if teeth cannot be saved
⚠️ Periodontitis requires immediate care. If untreated, it can lead to significant bone and tooth loss, affecting overall health.

Ancient Treatments

Ancient treatments for gum diseases like periodontitis included saltwater rinses, herbal poultices, and oil pulling. In Ayurveda, turmeric and neem were widely used for their antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. While they offered symptomatic relief, they were far less effective than modern therapies for reversing severe gum damage.

Natural Remedies for Periodontitis

Natural approaches may help manage symptoms and support gum health but should complement, not replace, professional care.

Diet and Lifestyle Approaches

  • Green tea for anti-inflammatory benefits
  • Vitamin C-rich foods to strengthen connective tissues
  • Clove oil to reduce gum pain
  • Oil pulling with coconut or sesame oil

Herbal and Food Remedies

  • Aloe vera gel for gum healing
  • Turmeric paste for antibacterial action
  • Neem mouth rinses for plaque control
  • Baking soda rinse for maintaining pH balance

Additional Measures

  • Massage gums gently to improve blood flow
  • Avoid excessive sugar intake

⚠️ While natural remedies reduce inflammation, they do not stop bone loss caused by periodontitis. Dentists should always be consulted for proper care.
