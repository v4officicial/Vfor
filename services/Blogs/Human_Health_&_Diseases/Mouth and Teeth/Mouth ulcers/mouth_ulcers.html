Mouth Ulcers | Painful Oral Sores

Mouth ulcers, also called canker sores or aphthous ulcers, are small painful lesions that form inside the mouth, on the tongue, gums, or lips. While they are usually harmless and heal within 1–2 weeks, recurrent or severe ulcers may indicate an underlying health condition and require medical attention.

Symptoms

Mouth ulcers vary in size, shape, and severity. Common symptoms include:

  • Round or oval white/yellow sores with a red border
  • Pain or burning sensation, especially while eating or talking
  • Single or multiple ulcers inside lips, cheeks, tongue, or gums
  • Swelling and discomfort in the affected area
  • Irritability in children due to pain
  • Secondary infection in severe or untreated cases

Causes

Mouth ulcers can develop due to multiple reasons, often linked to lifestyle or health issues. Common causes include:

  • Minor injuries (biting inside of cheek, dental braces, sharp teeth)
  • Stress and poor sleep cycle
  • Vitamin B12, folic acid, iron deficiencies
  • Hormonal changes (menstruation, pregnancy)
  • Allergic reaction to certain foods like nuts, chocolate, citrus, or spicy items
  • Weakened immunity or autoimmune disorders
  • Viral or bacterial infections
  • Certain medications such as NSAIDs or chemotherapy drugs

Precautions | Prevention

  • Maintain good oral hygiene and use a soft-bristled toothbrush.
  • Avoid spicy, acidic, and sharp-edged foods while ulcers heal.
  • Stay hydrated and include vitamin-rich foods in your diet.
  • Practice stress management techniques like yoga and meditation.
  • Check dental alignment and avoid sharp teeth or ill-fitting dentures.
  • Include probiotics like yogurt to balance oral bacteria.
  • Visit a doctor if ulcers last more than 3 weeks or are recurrent.

Treatment Options

Self-care and Lifestyle Remedies

  • Saltwater rinses or antiseptic mouthwash
  • Topical gels or ointments for pain relief
  • Avoid hot, spicy, and abrasive foods
  • Stay hydrated and adopt a balanced diet rich in vitamins

Medical Treatment

  • Topical corticosteroid gels for inflammation
  • Antimicrobial mouth rinses
  • Pain-relieving medications (local anesthetics)
  • Nutritional supplements for deficiencies
  • Treatment of underlying viral or bacterial infections
⚠️ Persistent, recurrent, or unusually large ulcers should be evaluated by a doctor to rule out more serious conditions such as oral cancer or autoimmune disorders.

Ancient Treatments

In Ayurveda and traditional Chinese medicine, herbal remedies like honey, turmeric paste, alum, and licorice root were used to soothe mouth sores. Oil pulling with coconut oil and rinsing with herbal decoctions were common practices for reducing inflammation and promoting faster healing.

Natural Remedies for Mouth Ulcers

Natural remedies for mouth ulcers can help soothe discomfort and accelerate healing.

Home Remedies

  • Rinse with warm saltwater 3–4 times daily
  • Apply honey, aloe vera gel, or ghee directly on the ulcer
  • Coconut oil application for its antibacterial properties
  • Chewing tulsi (holy basil) or licorice root for soothing effect

Dietary Support

  • Eat vitamin-rich foods (Vitamin B12, folic acid, iron)
  • Increase intake of probiotics for oral health
  • Consume turmeric milk for healing
  • Avoid oily and junk foods during ulcer occurrence

⚠️ Natural remedies provide symptomatic relief. Severe or chronic mouth ulcers should not be ignored, as they may indicate nutritional, immune-related, or precancerous conditions.
