Oral Thrush | Mouth Fungal Infection

Oral thrush, also known as oral candidiasis, is a fungal infection in the mouth caused by overgrowth of the Candida fungus. It appears as white, creamy patches on the tongue, inner cheeks, gums, or throat, and may cause discomfort, pain, or difficulty swallowing. Though typically mild, it requires prompt treatment, especially in infants, older adults, and those with weakened immunity.

Symptoms

Common signs of oral thrush include:

  • White, creamy patches on tongue, gums, or inner cheeks
  • Redness, irritation, and soreness under white patches
  • Pain or burning sensation while eating or swallowing
  • Cracking and redness at the corners of the mouth
  • Loss of taste or cotton-like feeling in the mouth
  • Bleeding when scraping the patches
  • In severe cases, spreading to the throat or esophagus

Causes

Oral thrush develops when Candida fungus multiplies beyond normal levels. Common causes include:

  • Weakened immune system (HIV, cancer, diabetes)
  • Prolonged use of antibiotics or corticosteroids
  • Wearing dentures, especially if ill-fitted
  • Poor oral hygiene practices
  • Dry mouth from medications or dehydration
  • Smoking and excessive alcohol use
  • Hormonal changes (pregnancy, hormonal therapy)

Children

  • Immature immune systems in infants
  • Frequent pacifier or bottle use without cleaning
  • Passing infection from breastfeeding mothers

Adults

  • Use of inhaled corticosteroids for asthma
  • Long-term antibiotic use
  • Lifestyle factors like smoking

Aged People

  • Reduced immunity and multiple health conditions
  • Dentures not cleaned properly
  • Xerostomia (dry mouth) due to medications

Precautions | Preventions

  • Maintain proper oral hygiene with regular brushing and flossing.
  • Clean dentures daily and remove them at night.
  • Avoid excessive sugar and yeast-containing foods.
  • Rinse mouth after inhaler use (if prescribed).
  • Stay hydrated to promote saliva flow.
  • Quit smoking and reduce alcohol intake.
  • Regular dental visits for early detection and care.

Treatment Options

Medical Treatment

  • Antifungal mouth rinses (nystatin)
  • Oral antifungal medications (fluconazole, itraconazole)
  • Topical treatments for infants and nursing mothers

Lifestyle & Oral Care

  • Brushing teeth twice daily and cleaning dentures
  • Avoiding sugary diet and alcohol-containing mouthwashes
  • Hydration and herbal mouth rinses for comfort
⚠️ Untreated oral thrush can spread to the esophagus and cause complications. A medical evaluation is necessary if symptoms persist.

Ancient Treatments

In traditional medicine, oral fungal infections were managed with saltwater gargles, turmeric water rinses, and chewing neem sticks for antimicrobial properties. These remedies provided partial relief but were not permanent cures.

Natural Remedies for Oral Thrush

Natural approaches may reduce discomfort and support antifungal treatment but should not replace medical care.

Home and Diet Approaches

  • Saltwater rinses to soothe irritation
  • Unflavored yogurt with probiotics to restore balance
  • Limit sugar and yeast intake

Herbal Remedies

  • Coconut oil pulling for antifungal effect
  • Tea tree oil rinses (diluted) for antimicrobial benefits
  • Aloe vera gel to soothe mouth lining

⚠️ Natural remedies support healing but prescribed antifungal medicines remain the primary treatment for oral thrush.
