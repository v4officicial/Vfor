Endophthalmitis | Severe Eye Infection & Inflammation

Endophthalmitis is a rare but severe inflammation and infection inside the eye’s aqueous and vitreous chambers. It most commonly occurs after eye surgery, penetrating injury, or infections spreading through the bloodstream. Immediate medical evaluation is critical to prevent permanent vision loss or eye damage.

Symptoms

Symptoms typically develop rapidly and may include:

  • Severe eye pain and redness
  • Blurred or loss of vision
  • Extreme light sensitivity (photophobia)
  • Eyelid swelling
  • Eye discharge or accumulation of white cells inside the eye (hypopyon)

Causes

Endophthalmitis can be divided into two categories by infection source:

  • Exogenous endophthalmitis: Infection enters the eye externally through eye surgery, intraocular injections, eye injury, or contaminated implanted devices.
  • Endogenous endophthalmitis: Infection spreads internally from bloodstream infections such as sepsis, abscesses, or other systemic infections.

Most cases are caused by bacteria, while fungi, and less commonly protozoa, may also be responsible.

Precautions | Prevention

  • Strict sterile techniques during and after eye surgery or injections
  • Immediate medical care for eye injuries
  • Quick management of systemic infections to avoid spread to the eye
  • Follow post-surgery care guidelines diligently
  • Regular eye examinations after surgeries or eye trauma

Treatment Options

Endophthalmitis demands urgent and aggressive treatment to try to save vision:

Medical Care

  • Intravitreal injections of broad-spectrum antibiotics and/or antifungals
  • Intravenous antibiotics for systemic infection control
  • Oral or injectable corticosteroids to reduce inflammation (as directed)
  • Topical antibiotic eye drops as supplementary therapy

Surgical Care

  • Pars plana vitrectomy to remove infected vitreous gel and reduce microbial load
  • Removal of infected intraocular devices if necessary
⚠️ Delay in treatment can lead to permanent vision loss, so prompt intervention is critical.

Types & Stages of Endophthalmitis

Endophthalmitis is subdivided into four main types depending on cause and timing:

  • Acute postoperative: Occurs within 6 weeks after surgery; rapid symptom onset; usually bacterial.
  • Chronic postoperative: Symptoms appear weeks to months post-surgery; often less aggressive bacteria or fungi.
  • Traumatic: Follows penetrating eye injuries; can be polymicrobial.
  • Endogenous: Infection spreads from bloodstream; associated with systemic infections.

Natural Remedies for Endophthalmitis

While endophthalmitis requires urgent medical treatment, supportive care includes:

  • Consuming antioxidant-rich foods like fruits and vegetables
  • Hydration to support immune function
  • Avoiding smoking and alcohol
  • Protecting eyes from further trauma or irritation

⚠️ Natural remedies are supplementary and do not replace urgent medical interventions.
