Uveitis | Inflammation of the Eye's Uvea

Uveitis is the inflammation of the uvea, the middle layer of the eye that includes the iris, ciliary body, and choroid. This condition can cause eye redness, pain, blurred vision, and light sensitivity. Timely diagnosis and treatment are essential to prevent complications like vision loss or permanent eye damage.

Symptoms

Symptoms of uveitis can vary with type and severity but typically include:

  • Eye redness and pain
  • Blurred or cloudy vision
  • Light sensitivity (photophobia)
  • Floaters (small dark spots floating in vision)
  • Decreased vision
  • Headaches or eye discomfort

Causes

Uveitis can arise from infections, autoimmune diseases, or eye injuries. Common causes include:

  • Autoimmune disorders (rheumatoid arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, psoriasis)
  • Infections (herpes simplex, herpes zoster, tuberculosis, syphilis, toxoplasmosis)
  • Injury or trauma to the eye
  • Eye surgery or exposure to chemicals
  • Idiopathic (unknown causes) cases

Precautions | Prevention

  • Seek prompt treatment for eye infections or injuries
  • Manage autoimmune and systemic inflammatory conditions
  • Avoid eye trauma and protect eyes during risky activities
  • Follow doctor’s advice for ongoing eye inflammation
  • Regularly attend eye check-ups for early detection

Treatment Options

Medications for Uveitis

  • Corticosteroid eye drops: To reduce inflammation, mainly used in anterior uveitis.
  • Oral or injectable corticosteroids: For intermediate, posterior, or severe cases.
  • Immunosuppressive drugs: For chronic or refractory uveitis to control immune response.
  • Antibiotics or antivirals: To treat infectious uveitis.
  • Eye drops to dilate pupil: To relieve pain and prevent iris adhesion.

Surgical and Other Treatments

  • Treatment of complications like glaucoma or cataracts if they develop.
  • Laser therapy or vitrectomy in severe cases.
⚠️ Early and consistent treatment is critical to prevent complications such as glaucoma, cataracts, macular edema, retinal detachment, and permanent vision loss.

Types & Stages of Uveitis

Uveitis is classified by the site of inflammation within the eye. Understanding the type helps guide treatment.

Types of Uveitis

  • Anterior Uveitis (Iritis): The most common form; affects the front of the eye (iris and ciliary body). Causes redness, pain, and light sensitivity.
  • Intermediate Uveitis: Inflammation of the vitreous humor and ciliary body, often causing floaters and blurred vision.
  • Posterior Uveitis: Affects the retina and choroid at the back of the eye; may cause vision loss.
  • Panuveitis: Inflammation affecting all parts of the uvea, the most severe form, potentially impacting the entire eye.

Stages of Uveitis

Stage Key Features Symptoms
Acute Sudden onset inflammation, often painful and red eye Redness, eye pain, photophobia, blurry vision
Chronic Persistent inflammation lasting months to years Recurring symptoms, possible vision deterioration
Recurrent Repeated episodes separated by periods of inactivity Repeated episodes of pain, redness, light sensitivity

⚠️ Early diagnosis and tailored treatment improve outcomes and reduce long-term vision complications across all uveitis types.

Natural Remedies for Uveitis

Natural remedies cannot replace medical treatment but may support eye health and immune balance during uveitis therapy:

Dietary Recommendations

  • Consume omega-3 rich foods like salmon and flaxseeds for anti-inflammatory effects
  • Eat antioxidants-rich fruits and vegetables to support immune health
  • Stay well hydrated and avoid smoking

Lifestyle Tips

  • Protect eyes from bright light with UV-blocking sunglasses
  • Rest eyes during flare-ups and avoid eye strain
  • Adhere strictly to prescribed medications

⚠️ Natural methods are supportive and should not replace prescribed immunosuppressive or anti-inflammatory therapies.
