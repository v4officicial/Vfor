Eye Infections | Common Types & Management

Eye infections affect various parts of the eye and can cause redness, swelling, pain, discharge, and blurry vision. They may be caused by bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites. Early recognition and treatment are crucial to avoid complications and preserve vision.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of eye infections include:

  • Red or pink eyes
  • Itching or burning sensation
  • Eye pain or discomfort
  • Swelling of eyelids or around eyes
  • Discharge, crusting on eyelashes
  • Watery or teary eyes
  • Blurred or decreased vision
  • Sensitivity to light

Common Types of Eye Infections

Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)

Infection or inflammation of the conjunctiva caused by bacteria, viruses, or allergens. Highly contagious when infectious.

Keratitis

Infection of the cornea causing pain, blurred vision, and sensitivity to light. Bacterial, viral, or fungal causes.

Stye (Hordeolum)

An infection of oil glands on the eyelid edge resulting in a painful, red bump.

Blepharitis

Chronic inflammation of the eyelid margins, often from bacterial infection or skin conditions.

Uveitis

Inflammation of the uveal tract inside the eye, can be infectious or autoimmune.

Endophthalmitis

Severe infection inside the eye, usually after surgery or trauma, and requires urgent treatment.

Causes

Eye infections are caused by different pathogens and factors:

  • Bacteria: Staphylococcus, Streptococcus, Pseudomonas, among others
  • Viruses: Adenovirus (pink eye), herpes simplex virus, varicella-zoster virus
  • Fungi: Candida, Fusarium (especially after injury or surgery)
  • Parasites: Rare but include Acanthamoeba, especially in contact lens wearers
  • Injury, contact lens misuse, poor hygiene, exposure to contaminated water

Precautions | Prevention

  • Wash hands frequently and avoid touching the eyes
  • Do not share towels, pillows, or eye cosmetics
  • Follow proper contact lens care and hygiene
  • Avoid swimming with contact lenses or in contaminated water
  • Maintain clean environments and avoid exposure to irritants

Treatment Options

Conjunctivitis

  • Bacterial: Antibiotic eye drops or ointments
  • Viral: Usually self-limiting; cold compress and lubricants
  • Allergic: Antihistamine and anti-inflammatory drops

Keratitis

  • Bacterial keratitis: Intensive antibiotic eye drops and sometimes oral antibiotics
  • Viral keratitis: Antiviral medications and eye drops
  • Fungal keratitis: Antifungal medications over weeks to months

Stye

  • Warm compresses multiple times daily
  • Topical antibiotic ointments if infected
  • Avoid eye makeup and contact lenses during infection

Blepharitis

  • Regular eyelid hygiene cleansing
  • Warm compresses and antibiotics if required

Uveitis and Endophthalmitis

  • Require urgent medical care with specialized medications and sometimes surgery

⚠️ Seek medical attention if symptoms worsen or vision changes occur. Untreated eye infections can lead to serious complications.
